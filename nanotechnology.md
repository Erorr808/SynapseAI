# Nanotechnology and Nanomaterials

Nanotechnology manipulates matter at atomic and molecular scales, typically 1-100 nanometers, enabling novel properties and applications.

## Nanotechnology Fundamentals

### Scale and Dimensions
- **Nanometer Scale**: 10^-9 meters, atomic dimensions
- **Surface-to-Volume Ratio**: High surface area effects
- **Quantum Effects**: Size-dependent property changes
- **Brownian Motion**: Random particle movement
- **Self-Assembly**: Spontaneous structure formation

### Key Properties
- **Mechanical Properties**: Strength, elasticity changes
- **Optical Properties**: Color, transparency alterations
- **Electrical Properties**: Conductivity modifications
- **Magnetic Properties**: Magnetic behavior changes
- **Thermal Properties**: Heat transfer characteristics

### Fabrication Methods
- **Top-Down Approach**: Bulk material reduction
- **Bottom-Up Approach**: Atomic/molecular assembly
- **Self-Assembly**: Spontaneous organization
- **Lithography**: Pattern-based fabrication
- **Chemical Synthesis**: Solution-based production

## Nanomaterials

### Carbon Nanostructures
- **Fullerenes**: Spherical carbon molecules (C60)
- **Carbon Nanotubes**: Cylindrical carbon structures
- **Graphene**: Single-layer carbon sheet
- **Carbon Nanofibers**: Elongated carbon structures
- **Diamond Nanostructures**: Ultra-hard carbon forms

### Metal Nanoparticles
- **Gold Nanoparticles**: Biomedical applications
- **Silver Nanoparticles**: Antimicrobial properties
- **Iron Oxide Nanoparticles**: Magnetic applications
- **Quantum Dots**: Semiconductor nanocrystals
- **Plasmonic Nanoparticles**: Light manipulation

### Ceramic Nanomaterials
- **Nanoclays**: Polymer composite reinforcement
- **Zirconia Nanoparticles**: Dental and structural applications
- **Titania Nanoparticles**: Photocatalytic properties
- **Alumina Nanoparticles**: Abrasive and thermal applications
- **Silica Nanoparticles**: Drug delivery systems

### Polymeric Nanomaterials
- **Dendrimers**: Branched polymer structures
- **Nanogels**: Cross-linked polymer networks
- **Nanofibers**: Electrospun polymer fibers
- **Nanocapsules**: Polymer shell enclosures
- **Nanocomposites**: Polymer-matrix materials

- **Photolithography**: Light-based pattern creation
- **Electron Beam Lithography**: High-resolution patterning

### Chemical Methods
- **Sol-Gel Process**: Solution-to-gel transformation

- **Atomic Layer Deposition**: Layer-by-layer growth
- **Electrodeposition**: Electrochemical material deposition

### Physical Methods

- **Sputtering**: Ion beam material ejection

## Nanoscale Characterization

### Microscopy Techniques
- **Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM)**: Surface imaging
- **Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM)**: Internal structure
- **Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM)**: Surface topography
- **Scanning Tunneling Microscopy (STM)**: Atomic resolution
- **Near-Field Scanning Optical Microscopy (NSOM)**: Optical imaging

### Spectroscopy Methods
- **X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy (XPS)**: Surface composition
- **Raman Spectroscopy**: Molecular vibration analysis
- **UV-Vis Spectroscopy**: Optical property measurement
- **Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR)**: Molecular structure
- **Mass Spectrometry**: Molecular weight determination

### Physical Characterization
- **Dynamic Light Scattering (DLS)**: Particle size distribution
- **Zeta Potential Measurement**: Surface charge analysis
- **X-ray Diffraction (XRD)**: Crystal structure analysis
- **Thermogravimetric Analysis (TGA)**: Thermal stability
- **Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC)**: Thermal transitions

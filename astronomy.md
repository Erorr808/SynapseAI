# Astronomy Fundamentals

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole.

## The Solar System

### Inner Planets
- **Mercury**: Closest to the Sun, no atmosphere, extreme temperature variations
- **Venus**: Hottest planet, thick atmosphere, retrograde rotation
- **Earth**: Only planet known to support life, 71% water surface
- **Mars**: Red planet, polar ice caps, potential for past water

### Outer Planets
- **Jupiter**: Largest planet, gas giant, Great Red Spot storm
- **Saturn**: Ringed planet, least dense planet, hexagonal storm at north pole
- **Uranus**: Ice giant, tilted on its side (98° axis tilt)
- **Neptune**: Windiest planet, deep blue color, Great Dark Spot

### Other Bodies
- **Pluto**: Dwarf planet, Kuiper Belt object
- **Asteroid Belt**: Between Mars and Jupiter
- **Kuiper Belt**: Beyond Neptune, source of short-period comets
- **Oort Cloud**: Spherical shell of icy bodies, source of long-period comets

## Stars and Stellar Evolution

### Main Sequence Stars
- **O-type**: Hottest, most massive, shortest lifespan (millions of years)
- **B-type**: Very hot, blue-white, short lifespan
- **A-type**: White, Sirius is an example
- **F-type**: Yellow-white
- **G-type**: Yellow, Sun is a G-type star
- **K-type**: Orange
- **M-type**: Coolest, reddest, longest lifespan (trillions of years)

### Stellar Life Cycle
1. **Nebula**: Gas and dust cloud
2. **Protostar**: Contracting gas cloud
3. **Main Sequence**: Stable hydrogen fusion
4. **Red Giant**: Expanded, helium fusion in core
5. **White Dwarf**: Remnant core (low mass stars)
6. **Neutron Star**: Dense remnant (massive stars)
7. **Black Hole**: Ultimate collapse (most massive stars)

### Supernovae
- **Type Ia**: White dwarf exceeds Chandrasekhar limit
- **Type Ib/c**: Core-collapse of massive stars
- **Type II**: Hydrogen envelope present during explosion
- Used as standard candles for measuring cosmic distances

## Galaxies

### Types of Galaxies
- **Spiral**: Rotating disk with arms, central bulge (Milky Way)
- **Elliptical**: Ellipsoidal, little gas/dust, older stars
- **Irregular**: No defined shape, chaotic appearance
- **Lenticular**: Disk but no spiral arms

### Milky Way Galaxy
- **Structure**: Spiral galaxy, 100,000-180,000 light-years diameter
- **Components**: Disk, bulge, halo, spiral arms
- **Location**: Solar System in Orion Arm, 25,000 light-years from center
- **Neighbors**: Andromeda Galaxy (closest large galaxy)

### Galaxy Clusters
- **Local Group**: Milky Way, Andromeda, 50+ smaller galaxies
- **Virgo Cluster**: 1,300+ galaxies, 50 million light-years away
- **Superclusters**: Largest structures, filaments of galaxy clusters

## Cosmology

### Big Bang Theory
- **Origin**: Universe began 13.8 billion years ago
- **Evidence**: Cosmic microwave background, galaxy redshift, abundance of light elements
- **Timeline**:
  - 10^-36 seconds: Planck epoch
  - 10^-6 seconds: Quark epoch
  - 3 minutes: Nucleosynthesis
  - 380,000 years: Recombination, CMB formation
  - 1 billion years: First stars form

### Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB)
- **Discovery**: Penzias and Wilson (1965)
- **Temperature**: 2.7 K (-270.4°C)
- **Significance**: Snapshot of universe at 380,000 years old
- **Anisotropies**: Tiny temperature variations show density fluctuations

### Dark Matter and Dark Energy
- **Dark Matter**: 27% of universe mass, gravitational effects observed
- **Evidence**: Galaxy rotation curves, gravitational lensing, CMB
- **Candidates**: WIMPs, axions, sterile neutrinos
- **Dark Energy**: 68% of universe energy, accelerating expansion
- **Evidence**: Supernova observations, CMB measurements

## Observational Techniques

### Telescopes
- **Optical**: Visible light telescopes (Hubble, Keck)
- **Radio**: Detect radio waves (Arecibo, ALMA)
- **Space-Based**: Above Earth's atmosphere (Hubble, James Webb)
- **Infrared**: Heat radiation detection
- **Ultraviolet/X-ray/Gamma-ray**: High-energy radiation

### Spectroscopy
- **Principle**: Light split into component wavelengths
- **Applications**: Chemical composition, temperature, velocity
- **Doppler Effect**: Redshift (moving away), blueshift (moving toward)
- **Emission/Absorption Lines**: Identify elements present

### Space Missions
- **Hubble Space Telescope**: Ultraviolet, visible, near-infrared
- **James Webb Space Telescope**: Infrared, first galaxies, exoplanets
- **Chandra X-ray Observatory**: High-energy X-rays
- **Parker Solar Probe**: Study Sun's corona
- **Voyager Golden Record**: Message to extraterrestrial life

## Exoplanets and Astrobiology

### Exoplanet Detection Methods
- **Transit Method**: Planet blocks starlight
- **Radial Velocity**: Star wobbles due to planet gravity
- **Direct Imaging**: Photograph planets directly
- **Gravitational Microlensing**: Light bending by gravity
- **Astrometry**: Precise star position measurements

### Habitable Zones
- **Definition**: Region around star where liquid water can exist
- **Factors**: Star temperature, planet distance, atmosphere
- **Examples**: TRAPPIST-1 system (7 Earth-sized planets)
- **Biosignatures**: Atmospheric indicators of life

### Astrobiology
- **Definition**: Study of life in the universe
- **Extremophiles**: Life in extreme Earth environments
- **Panspermia**: Life spreads through space
- **Drake Equation**: Estimate of communicative civilizations
- **SETI**: Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence

Astronomy helps us understand our place in the universe and the fundamental laws governing cosmic phenomena.

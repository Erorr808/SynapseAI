# Geography Fundamentals

Geography is the study of Earth's landscapes, peoples, places, and environments, examining spatial relationships and patterns.

## Physical Geography

### Earth's Structure
- **Lithosphere**: solid outer layer, tectonic plates
- **Hydrosphere**: water systems (oceans, rivers, lakes, groundwater)
- **Atmosphere**: gaseous envelope, weather and climate
- **Biosphere**: living organisms and ecosystems

### Landforms
- **Mountains**: formed by tectonic forces or volcanism
- **Plains**: flat or gently rolling terrain
- **Plateaus**: elevated flat areas
- **Valleys**: low areas between hills/mountains
- **Canyons**: deep, narrow valleys
- **Coastal Features**: beaches, cliffs, deltas, estuaries

### Water Systems
- **Oceans**: Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Arctic, Southern
- **Rivers**: drainage basins, watersheds, floodplains
- **Lakes**: freshwater vs saline, formation types
- **Groundwater**: aquifers, springs, wells
- **Glaciers**: ice sheets, alpine glaciers, icebergs

### Climate Systems
- **Tropical**: equatorial, monsoon climates
- **Dry**: desert, steppe climates
- **Temperate**: Mediterranean, humid continental
- **Continental**: subarctic, tundra climates
- **Polar**: ice cap climates
- **Highland**: mountain climates

## Human Geography

### Population Geography
- **Population Distribution**: density, concentration, dispersion
- **Population Growth**: demographic transition model
- **Migration Patterns**: internal, international, forced migration
- **Urbanization**: megacities, urban sprawl, suburbanization
- **Aging Populations**: demographic shifts in developed countries

### Cultural Geography
- **Language Distribution**: official languages, lingua francas
- **Religion**: major world religions and their geographic patterns
- **Ethnic Groups**: cultural hearths, diffusion patterns
- **Cultural Landscapes**: built environment reflecting culture
- **Cultural Conflicts**: ethnic tensions, cultural preservation

### Economic Geography
- **Primary Sector**: agriculture, mining, fishing
- **Secondary Sector**: manufacturing, construction
- **Tertiary Sector**: services, retail, tourism
- **Quaternary Sector**: information, research, education
- **Quinary Sector**: high-level services, government

### Political Geography
- **Nation-States**: sovereignty, territorial integrity
- **Borders**: natural, geometric, cultural boundaries
- **Geopolitics**: power relationships, alliances
- **Supranational Organizations**: EU, UN, NATO
- **Territorial Disputes**: contested regions

## Geographic Techniques

### Cartography
- **Map Projections**: Mercator, Robinson, Peters
- **Scale**: ratio of map to reality
- **Coordinate Systems**: latitude/longitude, UTM
- **Thematic Maps**: choropleth, dot density, flow maps
- **GIS**: Geographic Information Systems

### Remote Sensing
- **Satellite Imagery**: Landsat, SPOT, commercial satellites
- **Aerial Photography**: oblique vs vertical
- **LiDAR**: light detection and ranging
- **Radar**: weather radar, ground-penetrating radar

### Field Methods
- **GPS**: Global Positioning System
- **Surveying**: triangulation, leveling
- **Sampling**: systematic, stratified, cluster sampling
- **Participant Observation**: ethnographic methods

## World Regions

### Europe
- **Physical**: varied terrain, temperate climate
- **Cultural**: diverse languages, EU integration
- **Economic**: developed economies, trade hubs
- **Political**: democratic systems, NATO alliance

- **Physical**: Himalayas, monsoon climates, deserts
- **Cultural**: ancient civilizations, diverse religions
- **Economic**: emerging markets, manufacturing centers

- **Economic**: resource-rich, developing economies

- **Physical**: Rocky Mountains, Great Plains, varied climates

## Environmental Geography

### Natural Hazards
- **Earthquakes**: tectonic plate movements
- **Volcanic Eruptions**: lava flows, ash clouds
- **Floods**: river flooding, storm surges
- **Hurricanes**: tropical cyclones
- **Droughts**: water scarcity, desertification

### Climate Change
- **Global Warming**: greenhouse gas effects
- **Sea Level Rise**: coastal erosion, island nations
- **Extreme Weather**: heat waves, heavy precipitation
- **Biodiversity Loss**: habitat destruction, species extinction
- **Carbon Footprint**: human impact measurement

### Resource Management
- **Water Resources**: freshwater scarcity, irrigation
- **Mineral Resources**: mining impacts, sustainable extraction
- **Energy Resources**: fossil fuels, renewables
- **Food Security**: agricultural systems, GMOs
- **Forest Management**: deforestation, reforestation

## Urban Geography

### Urban Systems
- **Central Place Theory**: hierarchical settlement patterns
- **Urban Hierarchy**: world cities, megacities, regional centers
- **Urban Morphology**: concentric zones, sector model, multiple nuclei
- **Gentrification**: neighborhood revitalization

### Transportation Geography
- **Transportation Networks**: highways, railways, airports
- **Accessibility**: mobility patterns, transportation equity
- **Logistics**: supply chain management
- **Sustainable Transport**: public transit, cycling infrastructure

### Urban Planning
- **Land Use**: zoning, mixed-use development
- **Smart Cities**: technology integration
- **Sustainable Development**: green buildings, renewable energy
- **Urban Resilience**: disaster preparedness, climate adaptation

- **Scale Analysis**: local to global perspectives
- **Pattern Recognition**: identifying trends and relationships
- **Critical Thinking**: evaluating geographic information

- **Environmental Consulting**: impact assessments

Geography provides spatial understanding of our world, connecting human and physical systems.

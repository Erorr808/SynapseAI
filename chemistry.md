# Chemistry Fundamentals

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, composition, and transformations, forming the basis for understanding materials and life processes.

## Atomic Structure

### Basic Components
- **Protons**: Positive charge (+1), mass ≈ 1 u, in nucleus
- **Neutrons**: Neutral charge, mass ≈ 1 u, in nucleus
- **Electrons**: Negative charge (-1), mass ≈ 1/1836 u, orbit nucleus

### Atomic Number and Mass
- Atomic number (Z): number of protons
- Mass number (A): protons + neutrons
- Isotopes: same Z, different A
- Ions: charged atoms (cations +, anions -)

### Electron Configuration
- Energy levels and sublevels (s, p, d, f)
- Aufbau principle
- Pauli exclusion principle
- Hund's rule

### Periodic Table
- Groups (vertical): similar properties
- Periods (horizontal): increasing atomic number
- Metals, nonmetals, metalloids
- Periodic trends: atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity

## Chemical Bonding

### Ionic Bonding
- Transfer of electrons
- Electrostatic attraction between ions
- High melting points, conduct electricity when molten
- Examples: NaCl, MgO

### Covalent Bonding
- Sharing of electrons
- Molecular compounds
- Polar vs nonpolar bonds
- Lewis structures and VSEPR theory

### Metallic Bonding
- Sea of electrons
- Electrical conductivity
- Malleability and ductility

### Intermolecular Forces
- Hydrogen bonding
- Dipole-dipole interactions
- London dispersion forces
- Affect physical properties

## Chemical Reactions

### Reaction Types
- **Synthesis**: A + B → AB
- **Decomposition**: AB → A + B
- **Single Replacement**: A + BC → AC + B
- **Double Replacement**: AB + CD → AD + CB
- **Combustion**: hydrocarbon + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O

### Stoichiometry
- Mole concept: 6.022 × 10²³ particles
- Molar mass calculations
- Limiting reactants
- Percent yield

### Reaction Rates
- Factors affecting rate: concentration, temperature, catalysts
- Rate laws and reaction order
- Activation energy
- Collision theory

### Chemical Equilibrium
- Dynamic equilibrium
- Equilibrium constant (K)
- Le Chatelier's principle
- Acid-base equilibrium

## Acids and Bases

### Acid-Base Theories
- **Arrhenius**: Acids produce H⁺, bases produce OH⁻
- **Brønsted-Lowry**: Acids donate H⁺, bases accept H⁺
- **Lewis**: Acids accept electron pairs, bases donate electron pairs

### pH Scale
- pH = -log[H⁺]
- Acidic: pH < 7, Basic: pH > 7, Neutral: pH = 7
- pOH and pKw relationship
- Buffer solutions

### Titration
- Acid-base titrations
- Indicators and endpoints
- Equivalence point calculations

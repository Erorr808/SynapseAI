# Stock Market and Equity Investing

The stock market represents ownership in publicly traded companies and serves as a key component of the global financial system. This comprehensive guide covers market structure, investment strategies, analysis techniques, and trading mechanics.

## Stock Market Fundamentals

### Market Structure
- **Primary Market**: New securities issuance (IPOs, secondary offerings)
- **Secondary Market**: Trading existing securities between investors
- **Stock Exchanges**: Organized marketplaces for securities trading
  - New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)
  - NASDAQ
  - London Stock Exchange (LSE)
  - Tokyo Stock Exchange (TSE)
  - Shanghai Stock Exchange (SSE)

### Market Participants
- **Retail Investors**: Individual traders and investors
- **Institutional Investors**: Mutual funds, pension funds, hedge funds
- **Market Makers**: Firms providing liquidity by buying/selling securities
- **Broker-Dealers**: Intermediaries facilitating trades
- **Investment Banks**: Underwriting and advisory services
- **Regulators**: SEC, FINRA, exchange oversight bodies

### Types of Stocks
- **Common Stock**: Ownership shares with voting rights and dividends
- **Preferred Stock**: Fixed dividends, priority over common stock
- **Growth Stocks**: High growth potential, reinvest earnings
- **Value Stocks**: Undervalued relative to fundamentals
- **Blue-Chip Stocks**: Large, stable, dividend-paying companies
- **Penny Stocks**: Low-priced, speculative stocks

## Trading Mechanics

### Order Types
- **Market Orders**: Execute immediately at best available price
- **Limit Orders**: Execute only at specified price or better
- **Stop Orders**: Become market orders when price reaches trigger level
- **Stop-Limit Orders**: Become limit orders when stop price is reached
- **Trailing Stop Orders**: Stop price adjusts with market movements

### Trading Sessions
- **Pre-Market**: 4:00 AM - 9:30 AM ET (order entry only)
- **Regular Hours**: 9:30 AM - 4:00 PM ET (active trading)
- **After-Hours**: 4:00 PM - 8:00 PM ET (limited trading)
- **Extended Hours**: Electronic trading outside regular hours

### Settlement Process
- **T+2 Settlement**: Trades settle two business days after execution
- **Clearing**: Trade confirmation and obligation establishment
- **Custody**: Securities held in investor accounts
- **Payment**: Funds transferred between buyer and seller

## Investment Strategies

### Long-Term Investing
- **Buy and Hold**: Long-term ownership for capital appreciation
- **Dividend Investing**: Focus on income-generating stocks
- **Index Investing**: Tracking market indices through ETFs
- **Value Investing**: Buying undervalued stocks
- **Growth Investing**: Investing in high-growth companies

### Active Trading Strategies
- **Day Trading**: Opening and closing positions within same day
- **Swing Trading**: Holding positions for days to weeks
- **Position Trading**: Holding positions for months to years
- **Scalping**: Making numerous small trades for small profits

### Portfolio Management
- **Asset Allocation**: Distribution across stocks, bonds, cash
- **Diversification**: Spreading risk across different investments
- **Rebalancing**: Adjusting portfolio to maintain target allocation
- **Risk Assessment**: Understanding portfolio volatility and drawdowns

## Fundamental Analysis

### Financial Statement Analysis
- **Income Statement**: Revenue, expenses, profitability
- **Balance Sheet**: Assets, liabilities, equity
- **Cash Flow Statement**: Operating, investing, financing cash flows
- **Key Ratios**: P/E, P/B, ROE, ROA, debt-to-equity

### Valuation Methods
- **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: Stock price divided by earnings per share
- **Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio**: Market value vs. book value
- **Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio**: Market value vs. revenue
- **Dividend Yield**: Annual dividend divided by stock price
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**: Present value of future cash flows

### Economic Analysis
- **GDP Growth**: Economic expansion indicators
- **Interest Rates**: Impact on borrowing costs and valuations
- **Inflation**: Purchasing power and cost effects
- **Employment Data**: Labor market health indicators
- **Industry Trends**: Sector-specific growth drivers

## Technical Analysis

### Chart Patterns
- **Trend Lines**: Support and resistance levels
- **Head and Shoulders**: Reversal patterns
- **Double Top/Bottom**: Reversal formations
- **Triangles**: Continuation patterns (ascending, descending, symmetrical)
- **Flags and Pennants**: Short-term continuation patterns

### Technical Indicators
- **Moving Averages**: Trend identification (SMA, EMA)
- **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: Momentum oscillator
- **MACD**: Trend-following momentum indicator
- **Bollinger Bands**: Volatility-based bands
- **Stochastic Oscillator**: Momentum comparison

### Volume Analysis
- **Volume**: Number of shares traded
- **Volume Indicators**: On-balance volume (OBV), volume-weighted average price (VWAP)
- **Accumulation/Distribution**: Buying vs. selling pressure
- **Volume Profile**: Price levels with highest trading activity

## Risk Management

### Market Risk
- **Systematic Risk**: Market-wide factors (economy, interest rates)
- **Unsystematic Risk**: Company-specific factors
- **Beta**: Measure of stock volatility relative to market
- **Diversification**: Reducing risk through varied investments

### Position Sizing
- **Fixed Dollar Amount**: Consistent dollar investment per trade
- **Percentage of Portfolio**: Risk percentage per position
- **Kelly Criterion**: Mathematical position sizing formula
- **Volatility-Based Sizing**: Adjusting for stock volatility

### Stop-Loss Orders
- **Fixed Percentage**: Sell when price drops X% from entry
- **Support Level**: Sell at key technical support levels
- **Time-Based**: Exit after holding period regardless of price
- **Trailing Stops**: Adjust stop price as stock moves favorably

## Derivatives and Options

### Options Basics
- **Call Options**: Right to buy stock at strike price
- **Put Options**: Right to sell stock at strike price
- **Strike Price**: Price at which option can be exercised
- **Expiration Date**: Option expiration date
- **Premium**: Cost of the option contract

### Options Strategies
- **Covered Calls**: Selling calls against owned stock
- **Protective Puts**: Buying puts to hedge stock positions
- **Straddles**: Buying calls and puts at same strike
- **Spreads**: Combining multiple options positions
- **Iron Condor**: Selling out-of-money calls and puts

### Futures and Commodities
- **Stock Index Futures**: Futures contracts on stock indices
- **Commodities**: Agricultural, energy, metals futures
- **Leverage**: Futures provide high leverage
- **Hedging**: Using futures to hedge portfolio risk

## Behavioral Finance

### Cognitive Biases
- **Confirmation Bias**: Seeking information confirming beliefs
- **Anchoring**: Over-relying on initial information
- **Herd Mentality**: Following crowd behavior
- **Loss Aversion**: Fear of losses over potential gains
- **Overconfidence**: Overestimating abilities and knowledge

### Market Psychology
- **Greed and Fear**: Emotional drivers of market behavior
- **Momentum**: Trend-following behavior
- **Contrarian Investing**: Going against market sentiment
- **Market Bubbles**: Irrational exuberance leading to crashes

## Regulation and Compliance

### Securities Laws
- **Securities Act of 1933**: New securities issuance regulation
- **Securities Exchange Act of 1934**: Secondary market regulation
- **Investment Advisers Act of 1940**: Investment advisory regulation
- **Sarbanes-Oxley Act**: Corporate governance and accounting reform

### Market Surveillance
- **SEC Oversight**: Securities and Exchange Commission
- **FINRA Regulation**: Financial Industry Regulatory Authority
- **Market Manipulation**: Prohibited practices (pump and dump, front-running)
- **Insider Trading**: Illegal use of non-public information

# Transportation Systems

Transportation systems move people and goods efficiently across distances using various modes and infrastructure.

## Transportation Modes

### Road Transportation
- **Highways**: Limited-access high-speed roads
- **Urban Streets**: City road networks
- **Rural Roads**: Connecting smaller communities
- **Parking Systems**: Vehicle storage infrastructure
- **Traffic Management**: Signal and flow control

### Rail Transportation
- **High-Speed Rail**: 200+ km/h passenger service
- **Commuter Rail**: Suburban passenger transport
- **Freight Rail**: Bulk goods movement
- **Light Rail**: Urban electric transit
- **Maglev Trains**: Magnetic levitation technology

### Air Transportation
- **Commercial Aviation**: Scheduled passenger flights
- **Cargo Aviation**: Freight transportation
- **General Aviation**: Private and recreational flying
- **Airports**: Terminal and runway infrastructure
- **Air Traffic Control**: Flight safety management

### Maritime Transportation
- **Container Ships**: Standardized cargo transport
- **Bulk Carriers**: Raw material shipping
- **Passenger Ferries**: Short-distance travel
- **Cruise Ships**: Leisure tourism
- **Ports and Harbors**: Loading/unloading facilities

### Pipeline Transportation
- **Oil Pipelines**: Petroleum product transport
- **Natural Gas Lines**: Energy distribution
- **Water Pipelines**: Fresh water delivery
- **Slurry Pipelines**: Mining product transport
- **Infrastructure**: Pumping stations, monitoring

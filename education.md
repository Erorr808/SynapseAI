# Education Systems and Learning

Education encompasses the systematic process of acquiring knowledge, skills, values, and attitudes through teaching and learning.

## Educational Philosophy

### Learning Theories
- **Behaviorism**: Stimulus-response learning (Pavlov, Skinner)
- **Cognitivism**: Mental process focus (Piaget, Vygotsky)
- **Constructivism**: Knowledge construction (Dewey, Bruner)
- **Humanism**: Self-actualization emphasis (Maslow, Rogers)
- **Connectivism**: Networked learning (Siemens)

### Educational Approaches
- **Traditional**: Teacher-centered, structured curriculum
- **Progressive**: Student-centered, experiential learning
- **Montessori**: Child-led, hands-on activities
- **Waldorf**: Arts-integrated, developmental stages
- **Reggio Emilia**: Project-based, community involvement

## Education Levels

### Early Childhood Education
- **Preschool**: Ages 3-5, social skill development
- **Pre-Kindergarten**: Academic preparation
- **Kindergarten**: Formal school introduction

### Primary Education
- **Elementary School**: Grades 1-5/6
- **Core Subjects**: Reading, writing, mathematics

### Secondary Education
- **Middle School**: Grades 6-8, transition period
- **High School**: Grades 9-12, college preparation
- **Subject Specialization**: Advanced coursework

- **Standardized Testing**: College entrance exams

### Higher Education
- **Associate Degrees**: 2-year programs
- **Bachelor's Degrees**: 4-year undergraduate

### Adult Education

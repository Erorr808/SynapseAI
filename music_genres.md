# Music Genres and Artists

Music encompasses diverse genres, styles, and cultural expressions through sound and rhythm.

## Classical Music

### Periods
- **Baroque (1600-1750)**: Ornate, contrapuntal
  - Bach, Handel, Vivaldi
  - Harpsichord, violin concertos
- **Classical (1750-1820)**: Clarity, balance
  - Mozart, Haydn, Beethoven
  - Symphony, sonata forms
- **Romantic (1820-1900)**: Emotional expression
  - Chopin, Liszt, Wagner
  - Program music, virtuosity
- **Modern (1900-present)**: Experimentation
  - Stravinsky, Schoenberg, Cage
  - Atonality, electronic music

### Orchestral Instruments
- **Strings**: Violin, viola, cello, bass
- **Woodwinds**: Flute, oboe, clarinet, bassoon
- **Brass**: Trumpet, horn, trombone, tuba
- **Percussion**: Timpani, snare, cymbals
- **Keyboard**: Piano, organ, harpsichord

## Jazz

- **Dixieland**: New Orleans, polyphonic
- **Swing**: Big bands, danceable

### Legendary Artists
- **Louis Armstrong**: Trumpet, scat singing
- **Duke Ellington**: Composer, bandleader
- **Charlie Parker**: Bebop innovator
- **Miles Davis**: Multiple styles, trumpet
- **John Coltrane**: Saxophone innovator
- **Ella Fitzgerald**: Vocal improvisation

## Music Theory

### Elements
- **Rhythm**: Beat, tempo, meter
- **Melody**: Sequence of notes
- **Harmony**: Chord progressions
- **Timbre**: Tone color, instrument sound
- **Dynamics**: Loudness variations
- **Texture**: Layering of sounds

### Scales and Keys
- **Major Scale**: Happy, bright sound
- **Minor Scale**: Sad, melancholic
- **Pentatonic**: Five-note scales
- **Blues Scale**: Minor pentatonic with flatted fifth
- **Chromatic Scale**: All twelve semitones

### Chord Progressions
- **I-IV-V**: Most common in pop/rock
- **ii-V-I**: Jazz standard progression
- **Circle of Fifths**: Key relationships
- **Cadence**: Harmonic resolution

## Music Production

### Recording Process
- **Tracking**: Recording individual instruments
- **Overdubbing**: Adding layers
- **Mixing**: Balancing levels, panning
- **Mastering**: Final polish, loudness

### Digital Audio Workstation (DAW)
- **Ableton Live**: Real-time performance
- **Logic Pro**: Professional production
- **Pro Tools**: Industry standard
- **FL Studio**: Pattern-based composition
- **GarageBand**: Entry-level production

### Effects and Processing
- **Reverb**: Spatial enhancement
- **Delay**: Echo effects
- **Compression**: Dynamic control
- **EQ**: Frequency balancing
- **Distortion**: Harmonic enhancement

## Music Business

### Industry Structure
- **Major Labels**: Universal, Sony, Warner
- **Independent Labels**: Smaller, artist-focused
- **Publishing**: Songwriter rights
- **Distribution**: Physical/digital delivery
- **Streaming**: Spotify, Apple Music

### Copyright and Royalties
- **Mechanical Royalties**: Record sales
- **Performance Royalties**: Radio, live performance
- **Synchronization**: Film/TV placement
- **Sampling**: Using existing recordings

### Live Performance
- **Concert Tours**: Revenue generation
- **Festivals**: Multi-artist events
- **Venue Types**: Arenas, amphitheaters, clubs
- **Production**: Sound, lighting, staging

Music reflects cultural diversity, emotional expression, and technological innovation across human history.

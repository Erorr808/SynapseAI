# Philosophy Overview

Philosophy is the systematic study of fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, values, reason, mind, and language.

## Branches of Philosophy

### Metaphysics
- Study of the nature of reality
- Ontology: what exists?
- Cosmology: origin and structure of the universe
- Free will vs determinism
- Mind-body problem

### Epistemology
- Theory of knowledge
- What is knowledge?
- Sources of knowledge: reason, experience, revelation
- Skepticism and certainty
- Justification and belief

### Ethics
- Study of moral values and principles
- Normative ethics: how should we live?
- Metaethics: nature of moral judgments
- Applied ethics: specific moral issues

### Logic
- Study of valid reasoning
- Deductive vs inductive reasoning
- Syllogisms and arguments
- Formal logic and symbolic logic
- Fallacies and critical thinking

### Aesthetics
- Philosophy of art and beauty
- Nature of aesthetic experience
- Artistic value and interpretation
- Beauty as subjective or objective

## Major Philosophical Traditions

### Ancient Greek Philosophy
- **Pre-Socratics**: Thales, Anaximander, Heraclitus
- **Socrates**: Socratic method, "Know thyself"
- **Plato**: Theory of Forms, ideal state, allegory of the cave
- **Aristotle**: Golden mean, categories, causality
- **Stoicism**: Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, virtue ethics
- **Epicureanism**: pleasure as the highest good
- **Skepticism**: suspension of judgment

### Medieval Philosophy
- **Augustine**: Christian philosophy, free will
- **Aquinas**: synthesis of Aristotle and Christianity
- **Islamic Philosophy**: Avicenna, Averroes
- Faith and reason debate

### Modern Philosophy
- **Rationalism**: Descartes, Spinoza, Leibniz
- **Empiricism**: Locke, Berkeley, Hume
- **Kant**: Critique of Pure Reason, categorical imperative
- **Utilitarianism**: Bentham, Mill
- **Existentialism**: Kierkegaard, Sartre, Camus
- **Pragmatism**: Peirce, James, Dewey

### Eastern Philosophy
- **Hindu Philosophy**: Vedas, Upanishads, karma, dharma
- **Buddhist Philosophy**: Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, nirvana
- **Confucianism**: social harmony, filial piety, ren
- **Taoism**: wu wei, balance of yin and yang
- **Zen Buddhism**: meditation, enlightenment

## Key Philosophical Concepts

### Existence and Reality
- **Dualism**: mind and body are distinct (Descartes)
- **Monism**: everything is one substance
- **Materialism**: only matter exists
- **Idealism**: reality is fundamentally mental
- **Phenomenalism**: reality consists of sense data

### Knowledge and Truth
- **Correspondence Theory**: truth matches reality
- **Coherence Theory**: truth is consistency within a system
- **Pragmatic Theory**: truth is what works
- **Relativism**: truth depends on perspective
- **Skepticism**: doubt about certainty

### Ethics and Morality
- **Deontology**: duty-based ethics (Kant)
- **Consequentialism**: outcomes determine morality
- **Virtue Ethics**: character-based morality (Aristotle)
- **Relativism**: morality varies by culture
- **Absolutism**: universal moral rules

### Mind and Consciousness
- **Dualism**: mind separate from body
- **Physicalism**: mental states are physical
- **Functionalism**: mental states defined by function
- **Qualia**: subjective experience
- **Hard Problem**: why consciousness exists

### Free Will and Determinism
- **Libertarianism**: genuine free will exists
- **Determinism**: all events are determined
- **Compatibilism**: free will compatible with determinism
- **Fatalism**: destiny controls everything

## Contemporary Philosophy

### Analytic Philosophy
- Emphasis on logic and language
- Ordinary language philosophy (Wittgenstein)
- Philosophy of mind and action
- Metaethics and moral philosophy

### Continental Philosophy
- Phenomenology (Husserl, Heidegger)
- Hermeneutics (interpretation)
- Critical theory (Frankfurt School)
- Postmodernism (Derrida, Foucault)

### Philosophy of Science
- Scientific method and demarcation
- Theory change and paradigms (Kuhn)
- Realism vs anti-realism
- Philosophy of physics, biology, psychology

### Political Philosophy
- Justice and rights (Rawls)
- Liberty and equality
- Democracy and authoritarianism
- Global justice and cosmopolitanism

### Philosophy of Religion
- Existence of God arguments
- Problem of evil
- Faith and reason
- Religious pluralism
- Secularism and atheism

## Applications

### Ethics in Practice
- Bioethics: life, death, medical decisions
- Environmental ethics: human-nature relationship
- Business ethics: corporate responsibility
- Professional ethics: codes of conduct

### Philosophy in Society
- Education and critical thinking
- Law and justice systems
- Public policy and governance
- Technology and AI ethics

### Personal Development
- Meaning of life questions
- Happiness and well-being
- Self-knowledge and authenticity
- Decision-making frameworks

Philosophy provides tools for critical thinking, ethical reasoning, and understanding fundamental questions about human existence.

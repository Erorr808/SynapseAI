# American Civil War: Nation Divided and Reunited

The American Civil War (1861-1865) was the deadliest conflict in U.S. history, fought over slavery and federal authority, resulting in massive casualties and fundamental social transformation.

## Causes and Background

### Slavery Conflict
- Southern economy dependent on slavery
- Northern industrial and free labor
- Moral and economic divide
- Westward expansion tension
- Regional polarization

### Political Tensions
- States' rights vs. federal authority
- Sectional competition
- Southern fear of northern dominance
- Republican victory 1860
- Lincoln's election catalyst

### Secession Triggers
- South Carolina secession (December 1860)
- Other southern states follow
- Confederacy formation
- Seven states initially
- Jefferson Davis elected president

## Major Battles

### Battle of Gettysburg (1863)
- Largest battle
- Three days fighting
- Turning point victory
- Union halted Lee's invasion
- Enormous casualties both sides

### Battle of Antietam (1862)
- Bloodiest single day
- 23,000 casualties
- Union prevented southern victory
- Lincoln issued Emancipation
- State of Maryland secured

### Battle of Fort Sumter (1861)
- War's first battle
- Symbolic importance
- Confederate bombardment
- Union surrender
- Public shock and anger

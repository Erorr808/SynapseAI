# Mythology and Religions

Mythology encompasses traditional stories, beliefs, and cultural narratives that explain natural phenomena and human experience.

## Greek Mythology

### Major Gods
- **Zeus**: King of gods, thunderbolt, sky
- **Hera**: Queen of gods, marriage, women
- **Poseidon**: Sea god, earthquakes, horses
- **Demeter**: Agriculture, harvest, seasons
- **Athena**: Wisdom, warfare, crafts
- **Apollo**: Sun, music, prophecy, healing
- **Artemis**: Moon, hunting, wilderness
- **Ares**: War, violence, bloodshed
- **Aphrodite**: Love, beauty, desire
- **Hermes**: Messenger, trade, thieves
- **Hephaestus**: Smithing, craftsmanship, fire
- **Dionysus**: Wine, ecstasy, theater

### Heroes and Legends
- **Heracles (Hercules)**: Twelve labors, strength
- **Odysseus**: Cunning, Trojan War, Odyssey
- **Perseus**: Slayed Medusa, rescued Andromeda
- **Theseus**: Minotaur, Athens founder
- **Jason**: Golden Fleece quest, Argonauts
- **Achilles**: Trojan War hero, heel weakness

### Mythical Creatures
- **Minotaur**: Bull-headed man in labyrinth
- **Centaur**: Horse-human hybrid
- **Siren**: Bird-women who lure sailors
- **Sphinx**: Lion-bodied riddle-asker
- **Hydra**: Many-headed serpent
- **Pegasus**: Winged horse
- **Griffin**: Lion-eagle hybrid

## Norse Mythology

### Aesir Gods
- **Odin**: All-father, wisdom, war, poetry
- **Thor**: Thunder god, hammer Mjolnir
- **Frigg**: Prophecy, marriage, motherhood
- **Loki**: Trickster, shape-shifter, chaos
- **Balder**: Light, beauty, purity
- **Tyr**: War, justice, single-handed

### Vanir Gods
- **Freyr**: Fertility, prosperity, peace
- **Freyja**: Love, beauty, war, magic
- **Njord**: Sea, wind, fishing

### Cosmology
- **Yggdrasil**: World tree connecting realms
- **Nine Realms**: Asgard, Midgard, Jotunheim, etc.
- **Ragnarok**: Twilight of gods, end of world
- **Valhalla**: Hall of slain warriors

## Egyptian Mythology

### Major Deities
- **Ra**: Sun god, creator, pharaoh symbol
- **Osiris**: Underworld, resurrection, agriculture
- **Isis**: Magic, motherhood, healing
- **Horus**: Sky god, kingship, falcon
- **Anubis**: Mummification, afterlife
- **Thoth**: Wisdom, writing, moon
- **Bastet**: Protection, cats, home
- **Set**: Chaos, desert, storms

### Creation Myths
- **Heliopolis**: Ra-Atum creation
- **Memphis**: Ptah as creator
- **Thebes**: Amun-Ra as supreme god
- **Hermopolis**: Ogdoad of eight deities

### Afterlife Beliefs
- **Weighing of Heart**: Ma'at vs heart on scales
- **Book of the Dead**: Spells for afterlife
- **Mummification**: Preservation of body
- **Duat**: Underworld realm

## Hindu Mythology

### Trimurti
- **Brahma**: Creator god
- **Vishnu**: Preserver, avatars (Rama, Krishna)
- **Shiva**: Destroyer, ascetic, dancer

### Major Epics
- **Ramayana**: Rama's quest, dharma
- **Mahabharata**: Kaurava-Pandava war, Bhagavad Gita
- **Puranas**: Ancient histories and genealogies

### Avatars of Vishnu
- **Matsya**: Fish, saved Manu from flood
- **Kurma**: Tortoise, supported mountain
- **Varaha**: Boar, rescued Earth
- **Narasimha**: Man-lion, defeated Hiranyakashipu
- **Vamana**: Dwarf, measured universe
- **Parashurama**: Warrior, cleansed Earth
- **Rama**: Ideal man, dharma embodiment
- **Krishna**: Divine love, Bhagavad Gita
- **Buddha**: Enlightenment, compassion
- **Kalki**: Future avatar, end of Kali Yuga

## Abrahamic Religions

### Judaism
- **Torah**: Five books of Moses
- **Talmud**: Oral law commentary
- **Kabbalah**: Mystical tradition
- **Holidays**: Passover, Yom Kippur, Hanukkah
- **Covenant**: Agreement between God and Abraham

### Christianity
- **Old Testament**: Hebrew scriptures
- **New Testament**: Life of Jesus, apostles
- **Trinity**: Father, Son, Holy Spirit
- **Sacraments**: Baptism, Eucharist, etc.
- **Denominations**: Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant

### Islam
- **Quran**: Final revelation, 114 suras
- **Hadith**: Sayings of Prophet Muhammad
- **Five Pillars**: Faith, prayer, almsgiving, fasting, pilgrimage
- **Sharia**: Islamic law
- **Sects**: Sunni, Shia, Sufi

## Eastern Religions

### Buddhism
- **Four Noble Truths**: Suffering, origin, cessation, path
- **Eightfold Path**: Right understanding, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration
- **Nirvana**: Enlightenment, liberation
- **Karma**: Action and consequence
- **Reincarnation**: Cycle of rebirth

### Taoism
- **Tao Te Ching**: Laozi's teachings
- **Wu Wei**: Non-action, effortless action
- **Yin-Yang**: Balance of opposites
- **Simplicity**: Return to natural state
- **Alchemy**: Physical and spiritual transformation

### Shinto
- **Kami**: Spirits inhabiting natural world
- **Matsuri**: Festivals honoring kami
- **Torii**: Gateway to sacred spaces
- **Purity**: Ritual cleansing
- **Animism**: Spirit in all things

## Indigenous Mythologies

### Native American
- **Creation Stories**: Emergence from underworld
- **Trickster Figures**: Coyote, Raven
- **Totem Animals**: Spirit guides
- **Vision Quests**: Spiritual journeys
- **Oral Traditions**: Stories passed down

### Aboriginal Australian
- **Dreamtime**: Eternal creation period
- **Ancestral Beings**: Created landscape
- **Songlines**: Navigation and creation paths
- **Totems**: Spiritual connections
- **Corroboree**: Ceremonial gatherings

### African Mythologies
- **Oral Traditions**: Griot storytellers
- **Ancestors**: Revered spirits
- **Nature Spirits**: Forest and river deities
- **Creation Myths**: Various origin stories
- **Masks and Rituals**: Ceremonial practices

## Comparative Mythology

### Archetypes
- **Hero's Journey**: Monomyth pattern
- **Flood Myths**: Universal deluge stories
- **Creation Stories**: Various cosmogonies
- **Afterlife Journeys**: Underworld travels
- **Trickster Figures**: Boundary-crossers

### Symbolism
- **Water**: Life, purification, chaos
- **Fire**: Transformation, destruction, divinity
- **Earth**: Stability, fertility, grounding
- **Air**: Spirit, intellect, freedom
- **Light/Dark**: Good/evil, knowledge/ignorance

Mythology provides cultural frameworks for understanding the world, human nature, and the divine.

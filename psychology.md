# Psychology Fundamentals

Psychology is the scientific study of mind, behavior, and mental processes, encompassing both individual and group phenomena.

## Major Psychological Perspectives

### Biological Psychology
- **Neuroscience**: brain structure and function
- **Genetics**: hereditary influences on behavior
- **Hormones**: endocrine system effects
- **Neurotransmitters**: chemical messengers in the brain

### Cognitive Psychology
- **Information Processing**: how we acquire, process, store information
- **Memory Systems**: sensory, short-term, long-term memory
- **Attention**: selective focus and concentration
- **Problem Solving**: reasoning and decision making
- **Language**: comprehension and production

### Behavioral Psychology
- **Classical Conditioning**: Pavlov's experiments
- **Operant Conditioning**: Skinner's reinforcement theory
- **Behavior Modification**: changing behavior through consequences
- **Social Learning**: Bandura's observational learning

### Psychodynamic Psychology
- **Unconscious Mind**: Freud's iceberg metaphor
- **Defense Mechanisms**: repression, projection, denial
- **Psychosexual Stages**: oral, anal, phallic, latency, genital
- **Ego, Id, Superego**: structural model of personality

### Humanistic Psychology
- **Self-Actualization**: Maslow's hierarchy of needs
- **Person-Centered Therapy**: Rogers' client-centered approach
- **Existential Psychology**: meaning and authenticity
- **Positive Psychology**: well-being and flourishing

## Developmental Psychology

### Lifespan Development
- **Prenatal Development**: conception to birth
- **Infancy**: 0-2 years, attachment theory
- **Childhood**: cognitive, social, emotional development
- **Adolescence**: identity formation, puberty
- **Adulthood**: career, relationships, aging
- **Late Adulthood**: retirement, wisdom

### Theories of Development
- **Piaget's Stages**: sensorimotor, preoperational, concrete operational, formal operational
- **Erikson's Stages**: psychosocial crises throughout life
- **Kohlberg's Moral Development**: preconventional, conventional, postconventional
- **Vygotsky's Sociocultural Theory**: zone of proximal development

## Personality Psychology

### Trait Theories
- **Big Five**: openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, neuroticism
- **Myers-Briggs**: 16 personality types
- **Eysenck's PEN Model**: psychoticism, extraversion, neuroticism

### Psychodynamic Theories
- **Freud's Psychoanalytic Theory**: id, ego, superego
- **Jung's Analytical Psychology**: archetypes, collective unconscious
- **Object Relations Theory**: early relationships and internal representations

### Humanistic Theories
- **Self-Concept**: how we perceive ourselves
- **Self-Esteem**: overall sense of self-worth
- **Self-Efficacy**: belief in our ability to accomplish tasks

## Social Psychology

### Attitudes and Persuasion
- **Cognitive Dissonance**: discomfort from conflicting beliefs
- **Attitude Change**: central vs peripheral routes
- **Conformity**: Asch's line experiment
- **Obedience**: Milgram's shock experiments

### Group Dynamics
- **Group Decision Making**: brainstorming, groupthink
- **Intergroup Relations**: prejudice, discrimination, stereotypes
- **Social Identity**: in-group vs out-group bias
- **Leadership Styles**: authoritarian, democratic, laissez-faire

### Interpersonal Relationships
- **Attraction**: proximity, similarity, reciprocity
- **Love**: Sternberg's triangular theory
- **Conflict Resolution**: win-win vs win-lose
- **Communication**: verbal and nonverbal cues

## Clinical Psychology

### Mental Disorders
- **Mood Disorders**: depression, bipolar disorder
- **Anxiety Disorders**: GAD, panic disorder, phobias
- **Personality Disorders**: borderline, narcissistic, antisocial
- **Psychotic Disorders**: schizophrenia
- **Eating Disorders**: anorexia, bulimia

### Assessment and Diagnosis
- **DSM-5**: Diagnostic and Statistical Manual
- **Clinical Interviews**: structured and unstructured
- **Psychological Testing**: intelligence, personality assessments
- **Neuropsychological Evaluation**: cognitive functioning

### Psychotherapy Approaches
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**: changing thought patterns
- **Psychodynamic Therapy**: exploring unconscious processes
- **Humanistic Therapy**: unconditional positive regard
- **Family Systems Therapy**: relational dynamics
- **Group Therapy**: peer support and interaction

## Cognitive Neuroscience

### Brain Structures
- **Frontal Lobe**: executive functions, planning
- **Parietal Lobe**: spatial processing, attention
- **Temporal Lobe**: memory, language comprehension
- **Occipital Lobe**: visual processing
- **Limbic System**: emotion, motivation, memory

### Neural Plasticity
- **Synaptic Plasticity**: strengthening/weakening connections
- **Neurogenesis**: new neuron formation
- **Functional Plasticity**: brain reorganization after injury
- **Critical Periods**: sensitive developmental windows

### Consciousness
- **States of Consciousness**: wakefulness, sleep, dreaming
- **Altered States**: meditation, hypnosis, drug-induced
- **Self-Awareness**: theory of mind
- **Qualia**: subjective experience

## Applied Psychology

### Educational Psychology
- **Learning Theories**: behaviorism, cognitivism, constructivism
- **Motivation**: intrinsic vs extrinsic
- **Intelligence**: multiple intelligences theory
- **Special Education**: learning disabilities, giftedness

### Industrial-Organizational Psychology
- **Personnel Selection**: hiring and assessment
- **Work Motivation**: job satisfaction, engagement
- **Leadership Development**: training and coaching
- **Organizational Culture**: climate and values

### Forensic Psychology
- **Criminal Profiling**: offender characteristics
- **Eyewitness Testimony**: memory and accuracy
- **Competency Evaluation**: mental state assessments
- **Rehabilitation Programs**: offender treatment

### Sports Psychology
- **Performance Enhancement**: mental training
- **Team Dynamics**: cohesion and communication
- **Injury Rehabilitation**: psychological recovery
- **Peak Performance**: flow state

## Research Methods

### Experimental Design
- **Independent Variables**: manipulated factors
- **Dependent Variables**: measured outcomes
- **Control Groups**: comparison conditions
- **Random Assignment**: reducing bias

### Correlational Research
- **Correlation Coefficient**: strength and direction of relationship
- **Causation vs Correlation**: avoiding false conclusions
- **Multiple Regression**: controlling for confounding variables

### Qualitative Methods
- **Case Studies**: in-depth individual analysis
- **Ethnography**: cultural observation
- **Phenomenology**: lived experience exploration
- **Grounded Theory**: theory development from data

### Statistical Analysis
- **Descriptive Statistics**: mean, median, standard deviation
- **Inferential Statistics**: hypothesis testing, p-values
- **Effect Size**: practical significance
- **Meta-Analysis**: combining multiple studies

Psychology integrates science and practice to understand human behavior and mental processes.

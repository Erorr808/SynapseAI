# Scientific Discoveries and Breakthroughs

Scientific discoveries have transformed our understanding of the universe, from fundamental particles to cosmic scales. This comprehensive overview covers major breakthroughs across physics, chemistry, biology, astronomy, and other fields.

## Physics Discoveries

### Fundamental Particles and Forces
- **1897**: Electron discovery by J.J. Thomson - First subatomic particle identified
- **1898**: Radium and polonium discovery by Marie Curie - Radioactive elements isolated
- **1900**: Quantum theory foundation by Max Planck - Energy quantization introduced
- **1905**: Special relativity by Albert Einstein - Space-time relationship revolutionized
- **1911**: Atomic nucleus by Ernest Rutherford - Nuclear model of atom proposed
- **1913**: Atomic spectra explanation by Niels Bohr - Quantum model of hydrogen atom
- **1924**: Wave-particle duality by Louis de Broglie - Matter exhibits wave properties
- **1925**: Quantum mechanics by Werner Heisenberg - Matrix mechanics formulation
- **1926**: Schrödinger equation by Erwin Schrödinger - Wave mechanics foundation
- **1927**: Uncertainty principle by Werner Heisenberg - Measurement limitations defined
- **1932**: Neutron discovery by James Chadwick - Neutral nuclear particle found
- **1932**: Positron discovery by Carl Anderson - Antimatter particle identified
- **1938**: Nuclear fission by Otto Hahn and Fritz Strassmann - Atomic energy release mechanism
- **1947**: Parity violation by Chien-Shiung Wu - Fundamental symmetry breaking
- **1956**: Neutrino oscillation by Bruno Pontecorvo - Neutrino mass evidence
- **1964**: Quarks by Murray Gell-Mann and George Zweig - Fundamental particle constituents
- **1974**: J/ψ particle by Burton Richter and Samuel Ting - Charm quark evidence
- **1983**: W and Z bosons by CERN - Weak force carriers discovered
- **1995**: Top quark by CDF and DØ collaborations - Last quark discovered
- **2012**: Higgs boson by CERN - Mass-giving particle confirmed

### Cosmology and Astrophysics
- **1543**: Heliocentric model by Nicolaus Copernicus - Sun-centered solar system
- **1609**: Telescope invention by Galileo Galilei - Celestial observation revolutionized
- **1687**: Universal gravitation by Isaac Newton - Gravity law formulated
- **1916**: General relativity by Albert Einstein - Gravity as spacetime curvature
- **1929**: Expanding universe by Edwin Hubble - Universe expansion discovered
- **1948**: Big Bang theory by George Gamow - Universe origin theory
- **1965**: Cosmic microwave background by Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson - Big Bang radiation found
- **1967**: Pulsars by Jocelyn Bell Burnell - Rotating neutron stars discovered
- **1974**: Black hole X-ray binary by Louise Webster and Paul Murdin - Stellar black hole evidence
- **1998**: Dark energy by Supernova Cosmology Project and High-Z Team - Universe acceleration discovered
- **2015**: Gravitational waves by LIGO - Spacetime ripples detected
- **2019**: First black hole image by Event Horizon Telescope - Supermassive black hole visualized

### Condensed Matter and Materials
- **1908**: Superconductivity by Heike Kamerlingh Onnes - Zero resistance at low temperature
- **1930**: Quantum Hall effect by Klaus von Klitzing - 2D electron behavior
- **1956**: Transistor by John Bardeen, Walter Brattain, William Shockley - Semiconductor revolution
- **1957**: BCS theory by John Bardeen, Leon Cooper, John Schrieffer - Superconductivity explanation
- **1973**: High-temperature superconductivity by Johannes Georg Bednorz and Karl Müller - Ceramic superconductors
- **1985**: Fullerenes by Harry Kroto, Richard Smalley, Robert Curl - Carbon cage molecules
- **1991**: Carbon nanotubes by Sumio Iijima - Nanoscale carbon structures
- **2004**: Graphene by Andre Geim and Konstantin Novoselov - Single-atom thick carbon

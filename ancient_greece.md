# Ancient Greece: Birthplace of Western Civilization

Ancient Greece fundamentally shaped Western culture, philosophy, democracy, and science. This guide explores Greek civilization from its emergence through its classical golden age.

## Historical Periods

### Dark Ages (1100-800 BCE)
- Followed Bronze Age collapse
- Development of Greek language and culture
- Emergence of city-states (polis)
- Creation of Greek alphabet from Phoenician
- Expansion into Mediterranean

### Archaic Period (800-480 BCE)
- Development of polis system
- Olympic Games established (776 BCE)
- Colonization throughout Mediterranean
- Rise of military states like Sparta
- Emergence of democracy in Athens

### Classical Period (480-323 BCE)
- Golden age of Greek culture
- Dominance of Athens and Sparta
- Persian Wars and Greek victories
- Parthenon construction
- Peak of philosophy, art, and drama

### Hellenistic Period (323-146 BCE)
- Alexander the Great's conquests
- Spread of Greek culture to Asia
- Greek-Eastern cultural blending
- Scientific and mathematical advances
- Roman conquest and end of independence

## City-States and Politics

### Athens
- Birthplace of democracy
- Ruled by assembly of citizens
- Restricted citizenship to free males
- Led Delian League against Persia
- Center of culture and philosophy

### Sparta
- Military-focused society
- Oligarchic government with two kings
- Rigorous warrior training system
- Conquest of Helots (enslaved people)
- Rival power to Athens

### Other City-States
- Corinth: Commercial and cultural hub
- Rhodes: Naval and trading power
- Syracuse: Major Greek colony in Sicily
- Thebes: Briefly dominant power
- Confederation attempts and conflicts

## Philosophy and Intellectuals

### Pre-Socratic Philosophers
- thales: First natural philosopher
- Heraclitus: Theory of change and flux
- Pythagoras: Mathematics and harmony
- Parmenides: Being and reality
- Empedocles: Theory of elements

### Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle
- Socratic method of questioning
- Plato's theory of forms and ideals
- Plato's Republic: Political philosophy
- Aristotle's logic and empiricism
- Aristotle's politics and ethics

### Stoics and Epicureans
- Stoicism: Virtue through reason
- Epicureanism: Pleasure from simplicity
- Cynicism: Virtue through asceticism
- Skepticism: Questioning knowledge
- Influence on Roman philosophy

## Democracy and Government

### Athenian Democracy
- Developed by Cleisthenes and Pericles
- Citizens voting in assembly
- Sortition system for selection
- Limited to male citizens
- Revolutionary for the time

### Democratic Institutions
- Agora: Public assembly space
- Ostracism: Exile by popular vote
- Jury system for trials
- Legislative and executive functions
- Direct participation by citizens

### Legal System
- Written laws publicly displayed
- Trials before juries
- Defense and prosecution speeches
- Democratic justice system
- Influence on modern law

## Military and Warfare

### Hoplite Warriors
- Heavily armed infantrymen
- Bronze armor and shields
- Spear and sword combat
- Phalanx formation tactics
- Professional soldiers

### Naval Power
- Trireme: Three-tiered warship
- Athenian naval supremacy
- Control of Mediterranean trade
- Naval tactics and strategy
- Importance of maritime power

### Major Conflicts
- Greco-Persian Wars
- Peloponnesian War between Athens and Sparta
- Alexander's conquests
- Campaigns in Persia and Egypt
- Greek independence wars

## Art and Sculpture

### Classical Greek Art
- Idealized human form
- Perfect proportions and symmetry
- Nude male figures celebrating body
- Contrapposto stance for realism
- Stone carving techniques

### Famous Sculptures
- Discus Thrower: Famous athlete figure
- Winged Victory of Samothrace
- Venus de Milo: Classical beauty
- Parthenon sculptures and friezes
- Life-size and monumental works

### Pottery and Painting
- Red and black figure pottery techniques
- Illustrated mythological scenes
- Geometric patterns and designs
- Vase painting as major art form
- Frescoes in homes and temples

## Architecture

### The Parthenon
- Sacred temple to Athena
- Built on Athenian Acropolis
- Doric columns and perfect proportions
- Decorated with sculptures
- Model for neoclassical architecture

### Greek Architectural Elements
- Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian columns
- Pediments and friezes
- Temples oriented to cardinal directions
- Open-air design and colonnade
- Influence on Western architecture

### Other Structures
- Theaters for drama performances
- Stadiums for athletic competition
- Agoras as public gathering spaces
- Walls and defensive structures
- Gymnasiums for athletic training

## Theater and Drama

### Types of Drama
- Tragedy: Stories of fate and suffering
- Comedy: Humor and social satire
- Satyr plays: Comedic mythological adaptations
- Choruses providing commentary
- Elaborate costumes and masks

### Famous Playwrights
- Aeschylus: Tragedy pioneer
- Sophocles: Master of tragedy
- Euripides: Psychological drama
- Aristophanes: Comedy writer
- Works still performed today

### Dramatic Festivals
- City Dionysia festival
- Contest between playwrights
- Audience participation and voting
- Religious and civic importance
- Origin of theatrical competition

## Religion and Mythology

### Olympian Gods
- Zeus: King of gods
- Hera: Queen of gods
- Poseidon: God of the sea
- Athena: Goddess of wisdom
- Apollo: God of music and sun
- Artemis: Goddess of the hunt
- And others in the pantheon

### Mythology and Heroes
- Heracles: Strength and struggle
- Perseus: Courage and ingenuity
- Theseus: Athenian hero
- Achilles: Greatest warrior
- Odysseus: Cunning and perseverance

### Religious Practices
- Sacrifice to gods and goddesses
- Temple worship and prayer
- Oracle consultation at Delphi
- Mystery religions and cults
- Integration of mythology into daily life

## Science and Mathematics

### Mathematical Advances
- Pythagorean theorem
- Geometric proofs and axioms
- Prime numbers and number theory
- Irrational numbers discovery
- Foundations of formal mathematics

### Astronomy and Physics
- Geocentric model of universe
- Study of celestial motions
- Understanding of seasons
- Early physics principles
- Technology like astrolabes

### Medicine
- Hippocratic Oath and ethics
- Study of anatomy and physiology
- Treatment methods and surgery
- Herbal remedies and pharmacology
- Foundation of Western medicine

## Olympics and Sports

### Origins and Significance
- Started 776 BCE at Olympia
- Religious festival to Zeus
- Athletic competition between city-states
- Four-year cycle of games
- Symbol of Greek unity

### Events
- Running races of various distances
- Wrestling and boxing
- Discus and javelin throw
- Long jump with weights
- Chariot racing

### Athletes and Training
- Professional athletes
- Rigorous training regimens
- Celebrity status and sponsorship
- Crowns of olive for victors
- Often went on to political careers

## Daily Life

### Social Structure
- Free citizens at top
- Slaves and non-citizens below
- Women with limited rights
- Children trained for citizenship
- Class-based privileges and duties

### Food and Dining
- Olive oil in cooking
- Bread, cheese, and vegetables
- Wine mixed with water
- Fish and seafood common
- Symposiums as social gatherings

### Education
- Paideia: Primary education system
- Gymnastics and music training
- Rhetoric and philosophy
- Limited to male citizens
- Private tutors and schools

Ancient Greece's achievements in democracy, philosophy, art, and science established foundations for Western civilization that endure today.

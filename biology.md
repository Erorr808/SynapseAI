# Biology Fundamentals

Biology is the scientific study of life and living organisms, encompassing everything from microscopic cells to complex ecosystems.

## Cell Biology

### Cell Theory
1. All living organisms are composed of cells
2. Cells are the basic unit of life
3. All cells come from pre-existing cells

### Cell Structure
- **Plasma Membrane**: phospholipid bilayer with proteins
- **Nucleus**: contains DNA, control center
- **Mitochondria**: energy production (cellular respiration)
- **Endoplasmic Reticulum**: protein synthesis (rough) and lipid production (smooth)
- **Golgi Apparatus**: modifies and packages proteins
- **Lysosomes**: contain digestive enzymes
- **Vacuoles**: storage organelles
- **Cytoskeleton**: structural support and movement

### Cell Types
- **Prokaryotic Cells**: no nucleus (bacteria)
- **Eukaryotic Cells**: have nucleus (plants, animals, fungi, protists)

## Genetics

### DNA Structure
- Double helix structure (Watson and Crick)
- Nucleotides: adenine, thymine, guanine, cytosine
- Base pairing: A-T, G-C
- Genes: segments of DNA that code for proteins

### DNA Replication
- Semi-conservative replication
- Helicase unwinds DNA
- DNA polymerase adds nucleotides
- Leading and lagging strands

### Protein Synthesis
- **Transcription**: DNA → mRNA (nucleus)
- **Translation**: mRNA → protein (ribosomes)
- Genetic code: codons (3 nucleotides) specify amino acids

### Inheritance Patterns
- **Mendelian Genetics**: dominant/recessive traits
- **Punnett Squares**: predict offspring genotypes
- **Sex-linked traits**: X and Y chromosomes
- **Multiple alleles** and **polygenic inheritance**

### Mutations
- Point mutations: single nucleotide changes
- Frameshift mutations: insertions/deletions
- Chromosomal mutations: deletions, duplications, inversions, translocations

## Evolution

### Natural Selection
- Darwin's theory: survival of the fittest
- Variation within populations
- Differential reproduction
- Adaptation to environment

### Evidence for Evolution
- Fossil record
- Comparative anatomy (homologous structures)
- Embryology
- Molecular biology (DNA similarities)
- Biogeography

### Speciation
- Allopatric speciation: geographic isolation
- Sympatric speciation: reproductive isolation without geography
- Reproductive barriers: prezygotic and postzygotic

## Ecology

### Levels of Organization
- Individual → Population → Community → Ecosystem → Biosphere

### Energy Flow
- **Trophic levels**: producers, consumers, decomposers
- **Food chains** and **food webs**
- **Energy pyramid**: 10% energy transfer efficiency
- **Biomass pyramid**

### Nutrient Cycles
- **Carbon cycle**: photosynthesis, respiration, decomposition
- **Nitrogen cycle**: nitrogen fixation, nitrification, denitrification
- **Water cycle**: evaporation, condensation, precipitation
- **Phosphorus cycle**: weathering, absorption, sedimentation

### Population Ecology
- Population growth curves: exponential vs logistic
- Carrying capacity
- Density-dependent and density-independent factors
- r-selected vs K-selected species

### Community Ecology
- Competition: intraspecific vs interspecific
- Predation and herbivory
- Symbiosis: mutualism, commensalism, parasitism
- Succession: primary and secondary

## Human Biology

### Organ Systems
- **Circulatory**: heart, blood vessels, blood
- **Respiratory**: lungs, trachea, alveoli
- **Digestive**: mouth, stomach, intestines
- **Nervous**: brain, spinal cord, nerves
- **Endocrine**: glands, hormones
- **Immune**: white blood cells, antibodies
- **Skeletal**: bones, cartilage
- **Muscular**: muscles, tendons
- **Reproductive**: gonads, reproductive tract
- **Urinary**: kidneys, bladder
- **Integumentary**: skin, hair, nails

### Homeostasis
- Maintaining internal balance
- Negative feedback loops
- Positive feedback loops
- Thermoregulation, blood sugar control

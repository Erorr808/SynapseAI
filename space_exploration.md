# Space Exploration

Space exploration involves the discovery and exploration of celestial structures using advanced technology.

## History of Space Exploration

### Early Theoretical Work
- **Copernicus** (1543): Heliocentric model
- **Galileo** (1609): Telescope observations
- **Newton** (1687): Laws of motion and gravity
- **Herschel** (1781): Uranus discovery
- **Kant & Laplace**: Nebular hypothesis

### Rocketry Development
- **Tsiolkovsky** (1890s): Rocket equation pioneer
- **Goddard** (1926): First liquid-fueled rocket
- **von Braun** (1930s-1940s): V-2 rocket development
- **Korolev**: Soviet rocket designer

### Space Race Era (1957-1975)
- **Sputnik 1** (1957): First artificial satellite
- **Yuri Gagarin** (1961): First human in space
- **John Glenn** (1962): First American in orbit
- **Apollo 11** (1969): Moon landing
- **Apollo 13** (1970): Near-disaster mission
- **Salyut 1** (1971): First space station

### Post-Apollo Era (1970s-1990s)
- **Skylab** (1973): First US space station
- **Apollo-Soyuz** (1975): International cooperation
- **Space Shuttle** (1981): Reusable spacecraft
- **Mir** (1986): Soviet space station
- **Hubble Telescope** (1990): Space observatory
- **International Space Station** (1998): Orbital laboratory

### Modern Era (2000s-present)
- **Mars Rovers**: Spirit, Opportunity, Curiosity, Perseverance
- **Commercial Spaceflight**: SpaceX, Blue Origin
- **International Lunar Station**: Artemis program
- **Private Space Stations**: Axiom, Orbital Reef
- **Space Tourism**: Suborbital and orbital flights

## Space Environment

### Orbital Mechanics
- **Kepler's Laws**: Planetary motion principles
- **Orbital Elements**: Inclination, eccentricity, altitude
- **Launch Windows**: Optimal departure times
- **Delta-V Requirements**: Velocity change calculations
- **Hohmann Transfer**: Efficient orbital transfers

### Space Weather
- **Solar Wind**: Charged particle streams
- **Solar Flares**: Magnetic energy releases
- **Coronal Mass Ejections**: Plasma clouds
- **Radiation Belts**: Trapped charged particles
- **Cosmic Rays**: High-energy particles

### Microgravity Effects
- **Fluid Dynamics**: Surface tension dominance
- **Combustion**: Flame behavior changes
- **Crystal Growth**: Perfect crystal formation
- **Biological Effects**: Muscle atrophy, bone loss
- **Plant Growth**: Altered tropisms

# Environmental Science

Environmental science studies the interactions between physical, chemical, and biological components of the environment and human impacts.

## Ecosystems

### Ecosystem Structure
- **Producers**: plants, algae, photosynthetic organisms
- **Consumers**: herbivores, carnivores, omnivores
- **Decomposers**: bacteria, fungi, breaking down organic matter
- **Food Chains/Webs**: energy flow through trophic levels
- **Energy Pyramid**: 10% energy transfer efficiency

### Types of Ecosystems
- **Terrestrial**: forests, grasslands, deserts, tundra
- **Aquatic**: freshwater (lakes, rivers), marine (oceans, coral reefs)
- **Wetlands**: marshes, swamps, estuaries
- **Urban Ecosystems**: cities as human-modified environments

### Biodiversity
- **Species Diversity**: number and variety of species
- **Genetic Diversity**: variation within species
- **Ecosystem Diversity**: variety of habitats and ecological processes
- **Hotspots**: regions with high biodiversity and threat

## Climate and Weather

### Climate Systems
- **Greenhouse Effect**: natural warming mechanism
- **Carbon Cycle**: CO2 exchange between atmosphere, biosphere, oceans
- **Water Cycle**: evaporation, condensation, precipitation
- **Ocean Currents**: thermohaline circulation, surface currents

### Climate Change
- **Global Warming**: rising average temperatures
- **Causes**: greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation
- **Impacts**: sea level rise, extreme weather, biodiversity loss
- **Mitigation**: carbon capture, renewable energy, reforestation

### Weather Patterns
- **Atmospheric Circulation**: Hadley cells, jet streams
- **El Niño/La Niña**: Pacific Ocean temperature variations
- **Monsoons**: seasonal wind patterns
- **Hurricanes**: tropical cyclones, storm surges

## Natural Resources

### Renewable Resources
- **Solar Energy**: photovoltaic cells, solar thermal
- **Wind Energy**: turbines, offshore wind farms
- **Hydropower**: dams, run-of-river systems
- **Biomass**: biofuels, biogas, wood energy
- **Geothermal**: underground heat utilization

### Non-Renewable Resources
- **Fossil Fuels**: coal, oil, natural gas
- **Nuclear Energy**: uranium fission
- **Mineral Resources**: metals, rare earth elements
- **Groundwater**: aquifers, sustainable extraction

### Resource Management
- **Sustainable Development**: meeting needs without compromising future generations
- **Circular Economy**: reduce, reuse, recycle
- **Resource Efficiency**: doing more with less
- **Life Cycle Assessment**: environmental impact evaluation

## Pollution and Waste

### Air Pollution
- **Particulate Matter**: PM2.5, PM10 health impacts
- **Ozone**: ground-level vs stratospheric
- **Acid Rain**: sulfur and nitrogen oxides
- **Indoor Air Quality**: VOCs, radon, mold

### Water Pollution
- **Point Source**: identifiable discharge locations
- **Non-Point Source**: diffuse pollution (agriculture, urban runoff)
- **Eutrophication**: nutrient enrichment, algal blooms
- **Heavy Metals**: mercury, lead, cadmium accumulation

### Soil Pollution
- **Pesticides**: persistent organic pollutants
- **Industrial Chemicals**: solvents, heavy metals
- **Plastic Pollution**: microplastics, degradation
- **Soil Erosion**: loss of topsoil, desertification

### Waste Management
- **Solid Waste**: municipal, industrial, hazardous
- **Electronic Waste**: e-waste recycling challenges
- **Plastic Waste**: ocean pollution, microplastics
- **Composting**: organic waste conversion

## Conservation Biology

### Habitat Loss
- **Deforestation**: tropical rainforest destruction
- **Urban Sprawl**: habitat fragmentation
- **Wetland Drainage**: loss of ecosystem services
- **Coral Bleaching**: ocean acidification effects

### Invasive Species
- **Introduction Pathways**: accidental vs intentional
- **Ecological Impact**: competition, predation, disease
- **Control Methods**: biological, chemical, mechanical
- **Prevention**: border controls, early detection

### Endangered Species
- **IUCN Red List**: threat categories
- **Endangered Species Act**: US protection legislation
- **Captive Breeding**: zoo conservation programs
- **Habitat Corridors**: connecting fragmented habitats

## Environmental Policy

### International Agreements
- **Paris Agreement**: climate change mitigation
- **Kyoto Protocol**: greenhouse gas reductions
- **Montreal Protocol**: ozone layer protection
- **Convention on Biological Diversity**: biodiversity conservation

### National Policies
- **Clean Air Act**: air quality standards
- **Clean Water Act**: water pollution control
- **Endangered Species Act**: wildlife protection
- **National Environmental Policy Act**: environmental impact assessment

### Environmental Justice
- **Disproportionate Impact**: pollution in low-income communities
- **Indigenous Rights**: traditional land stewardship
- **Global Equity**: developed vs developing nations
- **Climate Refugees**: displacement due to environmental change

## Sustainable Development

- **Goal 3**: Good health and well-being

- **Goal 7**: Affordable and clean energy
- **Goal 12**: Responsible consumption and production
- **Goal 13**: Climate action
- **Goal 14**: Life below water
- **Goal 15**: Life on land

### Green Technologies
- **Renewable Energy**: solar, wind, geothermal
- **Electric Vehicles**: transportation decarbonization
- **Smart Grids**: efficient energy distribution
- **Green Buildings**: LEED certification, passive design

### Corporate Sustainability
- **Triple Bottom Line**: profit, people, planet
- **Carbon Neutrality**: net-zero emissions
- **Supply Chain Sustainability**: ethical sourcing
- **ESG Investing**: environmental, social, governance factors

## Environmental Education

### Public Awareness
- **Environmental Literacy**: understanding ecological systems
- **Climate Communication**: effective messaging strategies
- **Behavioral Change**: encouraging sustainable actions
- **Citizen Science**: public participation in research

### Education Programs
- **School Curricula**: integrating environmental topics
- **Outdoor Education**: experiential learning

- **Long-term Ecological Research**: LTER network

- **Environmental Impact Assessment**: project evaluation

Environmental science provides the knowledge and tools to protect and sustain our planet's ecosystems and resources.

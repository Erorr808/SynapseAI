# Mathematics Fundamentals

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantities, shapes, and patterns, providing the foundation for science, engineering, and technology.

## Number Systems

### Natural Numbers
- Counting numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, ...
- Used for counting discrete objects
- Properties: closure under addition and multiplication

### Integers
- Whole numbers: ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
- Include negative numbers and zero
- Closure under addition, subtraction, multiplication

### Rational Numbers
- Numbers that can be expressed as fractions: a/b where b ≠ 0
- Include terminating and repeating decimals
- Examples: 1/2, 3.14, 0.333...

### Irrational Numbers
- Cannot be expressed as finite decimals or fractions
- Examples: π (pi), √2, e (Euler's number)
- Transcendental numbers: cannot be roots of polynomial equations

### Real Numbers
- Include all rational and irrational numbers
- Form a complete, ordered field
- Used in continuous measurements

### Complex Numbers
- Form: a + bi where i = √(-1)
- Extend real numbers for solving equations
- Applications in physics, engineering, signal processing

## Algebra

### Basic Operations
- Addition: combining quantities
- Subtraction: finding difference
- Multiplication: repeated addition or scaling
- Division: splitting into equal parts

### Equations and Inequalities
- Linear equations: ax + b = 0
- Quadratic equations: ax² + bx + c = 0
- Systems of equations
- Linear inequalities: ax + b > 0

### Functions
- Relation between inputs and outputs
- Domain and range
- Types: linear, quadratic, exponential, logarithmic
- Composition and inverse functions

### Polynomials
- Expressions with variables and coefficients
- Degree: highest power of variable
- Factoring and roots
- Synthetic division

## Geometry

- Angles: acute, obtuse, right, straight

- Pythagorean theorem: a² + b² = c²

### Coordinate Geometry

### Trigonometry

### Analytic Geometry

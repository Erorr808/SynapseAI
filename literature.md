# Literature and Authors

Literature encompasses written works of artistic merit, including novels, poetry, plays, and essays.

## Literary Genres

### Fiction
- **Literary Fiction**: Character-driven, realistic, artistic merit
- **Genre Fiction**: Mystery, romance, science fiction, fantasy
- **Historical Fiction**: Set in past with fictional characters
- **Magical Realism**: Magical elements in realistic settings
- **Gothic Fiction**: Dark, mysterious, supernatural elements

### Poetry
- **Lyric Poetry**: Expresses personal emotions
- **Narrative Poetry**: Tells a story
- **Epic Poetry**: Long heroic tales
- **Haiku**: Japanese 5-7-5 syllable form
- **Free Verse**: No regular rhyme or meter

### Drama
- **Tragedy**: Protagonist downfall due to flaw
- **Comedy**: Light-hearted, happy ending
- **Tragicomedy**: Mix of serious and comic elements
- **Absurdist Drama**: Illogical, meaningless situations
- **One-Act Plays**: Short, single-act performances

## Major Literary Periods

### Ancient Literature
- **Epic of Gilgamesh**: Oldest known literature (Mesopotamia)
- **Homer's Iliad and Odyssey**: Greek heroic poetry
- **Mahabharata and Ramayana**: Indian epics
- **Confucian Classics**: Chinese philosophical texts
- **The Bible**: Collection of religious texts

### Classical Literature
- **Greek Tragedy**: Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides
- **Roman Literature**: Virgil's Aeneid, Ovid's Metamorphoses
- **Chinese Poetry**: Tang Dynasty masters
- **Japanese Literature**: Tale of Genji (Murasaki Shikibu)

### Medieval Literature
- **Chanson de Roland**: French epic poem
- **The Canterbury Tales**: Chaucer's pilgrim stories
- **Dante's Divine Comedy**: Journey through afterlife
- **Beowulf**: Anglo-Saxon heroic poem
- **Arabian Nights**: Collection of folk tales

### Renaissance Literature
- **Shakespeare**: Tragedies, comedies, sonnets
- **Milton's Paradise Lost**: Epic Christian poem
- **Cervantes' Don Quixote**: First modern novel
- **Rabelais' Gargantua and Pantagruel**: Satirical novels

### Enlightenment Literature
- **Voltaire's Candide**: Satire of optimism
- **Swift's Gulliver's Travels**: Political satire
- **Defoe's Robinson Crusoe**: Adventure novel
- **Fielding's Tom Jones**: Picaresque novel

### Romantic Literature
- **Wordsworth and Coleridge**: Lyrical Ballads
- **Byron, Shelley, Keats**: English Romantic poets
- **Goethe's Faust**: German dramatic poem
- **Hawthorne's Scarlet Letter**: American Gothic novel

### Victorian Literature
- **Dickens' Novels**: Social commentary
- **Bronte Sisters**: Jane Eyre, Wuthering Heights
- **Hardy's Tess of the d'Urbervilles**: Rural tragedy
- **Stevenson's Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde**: Psychological horror

### Modern Literature
- **Joyce's Ulysses**: Stream of consciousness
- **Woolf's Mrs. Dalloway**: Interior monologue
- **Kafka's Metamorphosis**: Absurdist fiction
- **Hemingway's The Old Man and the Sea**: Minimalist style

### Contemporary Literature
- **Postmodern Novels**: Metafiction, intertextuality
- **Magical Realism**: Garcia Marquez, Rushdie
- **Graphic Novels**: Visual storytelling
- **Digital Literature**: Hypertext, interactive fiction

## Literary Devices

### Figurative Language
- **Metaphor**: Direct comparison without like/as
- **Simile**: Comparison using like/as
- **Personification**: Giving human qualities to non-human things
- **Hyperbole**: Deliberate exaggeration
- **Irony**: Contrast between expectation and reality

### Sound Devices
- **Alliteration**: Repetition of initial consonant sounds
- **Assonance**: Repetition of vowel sounds
- **Consonance**: Repetition of consonant sounds
- **Onomatopoeia**: Words that imitate sounds
- **Rhyme**: Similar sounding words

### Structural Devices
- **Foreshadowing**: Hints about future events
- **Flashback**: Interruption with past events
- **Allusion**: Reference to other works/literature
- **Symbolism**: Objects representing abstract ideas
- **Motif**: Recurring element with symbolic significance

## Literary Criticism

### Critical Approaches
- **Formalist**: Focus on text structure and language
- **Reader-Response**: Reader's interpretation
- **Structuralist**: Underlying patterns and systems
- **Poststructuralist**: Deconstruction of meaning
- **Feminist Criticism**: Gender representation

### Literary Theory
- **Marxist**: Class struggle and economic factors
- **Psychoanalytic**: Unconscious motivations
- **Postcolonial**: Colonialism's impact on literature
- **Ecocriticism**: Environment and nature themes
- **Cultural Studies**: Literature in cultural context

## World Literature

### African Literature
- **Oral Traditions**: Griots and storytelling
- **Chinua Achebe's Things Fall Apart**: Colonial impact
- **Wole Soyinka**: Nobel Prize winner
- **Postcolonial Novels**: Themes of identity and independence

### Asian Literature
- **Classical Chinese**: Poetry and philosophy
- **Japanese Literature**: Haiku, modern novels
- **Indian Literature**: Epics, modern writers like Tagore
- **Middle Eastern**: Arabian Nights, modern poetry

### Latin American Literature
- **Magical Realism**: Garcia Marquez, Borges
- **Boom Generation**: Cortazar, Fuentes
- **Testimonial Literature**: Personal accounts of oppression
- **Contemporary Poetry**: Neruda, Paz

### Indigenous Literature
- **Oral Traditions**: Myths and legends
- **Native American**: Creation stories, trickster tales
- **Aboriginal Literature**: Dreamtime stories
- **Contemporary Voices**: Sherman Alexie, Thomas King

## Literary Awards

### Nobel Prize in Literature
- **Established**: 1901 by Alfred Nobel
- **Criteria**: Outstanding literary work, idealistic tendency
- **Notable Winners**: Hemingway, Solzhenitsyn, Morrison

### Pulitzer Prize
- **Categories**: Fiction, Drama, Poetry, Biography
- **American Focus**: US literature and journalism
- **Notable Winners**: Faulkner, Steinbeck, Roth

### Booker Prize
- **British Origin**: Now international
- **Criteria**: Best novel written in English
- **Notable Winners**: Rushdie, Atwood, Ishiguro

### Other Awards
- **National Book Award**: American literature
- **Costa Book Award**: UK popular fiction
- **PEN Awards**: International literary recognition

Literature reflects human experience, culture, and imagination across time and societies.

# Business and Economics

Business involves creating, managing, and growing organizations that provide goods and services, while economics studies how societies allocate scarce resources.

## Business Fundamentals

### Business Types
- **Sole Proprietorship**: single owner, unlimited liability
- **Partnership**: shared ownership, shared liability
- **Corporation**: legal entity, limited liability, shareholders
- **Limited Liability Company (LLC)**: combines partnership and corporate features
- **Nonprofit**: social benefit, tax-exempt

### Business Functions
- **Marketing**: product, price, place, promotion (4Ps)
- **Operations**: production and service delivery
- **Finance**: capital management and investment
- **Human Resources**: employee management and development
- **Information Technology**: digital infrastructure and data management

### Entrepreneurship
- **Opportunity Recognition**: identifying market needs
- **Business Planning**: feasibility analysis and strategy
- **Funding**: bootstrapping, venture capital, loans
- **Scaling**: growth strategies and expansion
- **Exit Strategies**: IPO, acquisition, sale

## Economics Principles

### Microeconomics
- **Supply and Demand**: market equilibrium
- **Elasticity**: price sensitivity of quantity demanded/supplied
- **Consumer Theory**: utility maximization
- **Producer Theory**: profit maximization
- **Market Structures**: perfect competition, monopoly, oligopoly

### Macroeconomics
- **Gross Domestic Product (GDP)**: total economic output
- **Inflation**: general price level changes
- **Unemployment**: labor market conditions
- **Fiscal Policy**: government spending and taxation
- **Monetary Policy**: central bank interest rates and money supply

### Economic Systems
- **Capitalism**: private ownership, market allocation
- **Socialism**: government ownership, planned allocation
- **Mixed Economy**: combination of market and government intervention
- **Command Economy**: centralized planning
- **Traditional Economy**: customs and habits

## Financial Management

### Accounting Principles
- **Financial Statements**: income statement, balance sheet, cash flow
- **GAAP/IFRS**: accounting standards
- **Double-Entry Bookkeeping**: debits and credits
- **Accrual vs Cash Basis**: timing of revenue/expense recognition

### Corporate Finance
- **Capital Budgeting**: investment decision making
- **Cost of Capital**: required return on investments
- **Capital Structure**: debt vs equity financing
- **Dividend Policy**: payout vs retention decisions
- **Mergers and Acquisitions**: corporate combinations

### Investment Analysis
- **Time Value of Money**: present vs future value
- **Risk and Return**: portfolio theory
- **Asset Pricing Models**: CAPM, arbitrage pricing
- **Valuation Methods**: DCF, multiples, real options

## Marketing and Sales

- **Market Research**: consumer insights and analysis
- **Segmentation**: dividing markets into groups

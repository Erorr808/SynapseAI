# Industrial Revolution: Transformation of Society and Economy

The Industrial Revolution marked humanity's shift from agrarian societies to industrial, mechanized production. Beginning in Britain in the late 18th century, it spread globally, fundamentally transforming economy, society, and culture.

## Historical Timeline

### First Industrial Revolution (1760-1840)
- Began in Britain with textile industry
- Mechanization of production
- Steam engine development by James Watt
- Factory system emergence
- Rapid urbanization

### Second Industrial Revolution (1870-1914)
- Steel and rail expansion
- Chemical and petroleum industries
- Electrical power generation
- Mass production techniques
- Global spread of industrialization

### Post-Industrial Developments
- Service economy growth
- Information technology
- Automation and robots
- Global supply chains
- Digital transformation

## Technological Innovations

### Power Sources
- Steam engine revolutionized manufacturing
- Coal as primary energy source
- Factory-based centralization
- Transportation powered by steam
- Continuous operation capability

### Textile Industry
- Spinning jenny: Multiple spindles
- Water frame: Machine weaving
- Power loom: Fully mechanized
- Production increased exponentially
- Factory labor system

### Iron and Steel
- Blast furnace improvements
- Coke smelting process
- Rolling mills for production
- Bessemer process innovations
- Foundation for construction and machinery

### Transportation
- Steam locomotives on railways
- Canal construction for commerce
- Steamships for ocean travel
- Infrastructure expansion rapidly
- Connection of distant regions

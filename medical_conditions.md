# Medical Conditions and Diseases

This comprehensive guide covers major medical conditions, their causes, symptoms, diagnosis, and treatment approaches across various categories of health conditions.

## Infectious Diseases

### Bacterial Infections
- **Tuberculosis (TB)**: Caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. Symptoms include persistent cough, weight loss, fever, night sweats. Diagnosed by chest X-ray, sputum tests. Treated with antibiotics (RIPE regimen: Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol) for 6-9 months.
- **Strep Throat**: Streptococcus pyogenes infection. Symptoms: sore throat, fever, swollen glands, white patches. Diagnosed by throat swab. Treated with penicillin or amoxicillin.
- **Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)**: Usually E. coli bacteria. Symptoms: frequent urination, burning sensation, cloudy urine. Diagnosed by urine culture. Treated with antibiotics like trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole.
- **Pneumonia**: Various bacterial causes (Streptococcus pneumoniae most common). Symptoms: cough, fever, chest pain, shortness of breath. Diagnosed by chest X-ray, sputum culture. Treated with antibiotics.
- **Cellulitis**: Skin infection by Staphylococcus or Streptococcus. Symptoms: red, swollen, painful skin. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with oral antibiotics.
- **Lyme Disease**: Borrelia burgdorferi transmitted by ticks. Symptoms: bull's-eye rash, fever, fatigue, joint pain. Diagnosed by ELISA test. Treated with doxycycline.

### Viral Infections
- **Influenza (Flu)**: Influenza viruses. Symptoms: fever, cough, body aches, fatigue. Diagnosed by rapid tests. Treated with antivirals like oseltamivir, rest, fluids.
- **COVID-19**: SARS-CoV-2 virus. Symptoms: fever, cough, loss of taste/smell, fatigue. Diagnosed by PCR tests. Treated with antivirals, monoclonal antibodies, supportive care.
- **HIV/AIDS**: Human immunodeficiency virus. Symptoms: flu-like illness initially, then opportunistic infections. Diagnosed by antibody tests. Treated with antiretroviral therapy (ART).
- **Hepatitis B**: HBV virus affecting liver. Symptoms: jaundice, fatigue, abdominal pain. Diagnosed by blood tests. Treated with antivirals like tenofovir.
- **Herpes Simplex**: HSV-1 (oral) and HSV-2 (genital). Symptoms: painful sores, fever. Diagnosed by viral culture. Treated with acyclovir.
- **Chickenpox**: Varicella-zoster virus. Symptoms: itchy rash, fever. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with acyclovir, calamine lotion.

### Fungal Infections
- **Candidiasis (Yeast Infection)**: Candida albicans. Symptoms: itching, discharge, redness. Diagnosed by microscopic exam. Treated with antifungal creams or oral medications.
- **Athlete's Foot**: Trichophyton fungus. Symptoms: itchy, scaly skin between toes. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with antifungal creams.
- **Ringworm**: Various dermatophytes. Symptoms: circular, itchy rash. Diagnosed by skin scraping. Treated with antifungal medications.

### Parasitic Infections
- **Malaria**: Plasmodium parasites transmitted by mosquitoes. Symptoms: fever, chills, sweating, anemia. Diagnosed by blood smear. Treated with antimalarials like chloroquine.
- **Giardia**: Giardia lamblia parasite. Symptoms: diarrhea, abdominal pain, bloating. Diagnosed by stool tests. Treated with metronidazole.
- **Pinworms**: Enterobius vermicularis. Symptoms: anal itching, especially at night. Diagnosed by tape test. Treated with mebendazole.

## Cardiovascular Diseases

### Heart Conditions
- **Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)**: Atherosclerosis of coronary arteries. Symptoms: chest pain (angina), shortness of breath. Diagnosed by ECG, stress tests, angiography. Treated with medications, angioplasty, bypass surgery.
- **Heart Failure**: Heart unable to pump effectively. Symptoms: fatigue, swelling, shortness of breath. Diagnosed by echocardiogram, BNP levels. Treated with ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, diuretics.
- **Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)**: Irregular heartbeat. Symptoms: palpitations, fatigue, dizziness. Diagnosed by ECG. Treated with anticoagulants, antiarrhythmics, cardioversion.
- **Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)**: Blood pressure >130/80 mmHg. Often asymptomatic. Diagnosed by sphygmomanometer. Treated with lifestyle changes, medications (ACE inhibitors, diuretics).
- **Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack)**: Coronary artery blockage. Symptoms: chest pain, sweating, nausea. Diagnosed by ECG, cardiac enzymes. Treated with aspirin, thrombolytics, angioplasty.

### Vascular Conditions
- **Stroke**: Brain blood vessel blockage or rupture. Symptoms: sudden weakness, confusion, vision changes. Diagnosed by CT/MRI. Treated with clot-busting drugs, rehabilitation.
- **Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)**: Atherosclerosis in leg arteries. Symptoms: leg pain when walking. Diagnosed by ankle-brachial index. Treated with exercise, medications, angioplasty.
- **Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)**: Blood clot in deep veins. Symptoms: leg swelling, pain. Diagnosed by ultrasound. Treated with anticoagulants.
- **Pulmonary Embolism**: Blood clot in lung arteries. Symptoms: shortness of breath, chest pain. Diagnosed by CT angiogram. Treated with anticoagulants, thrombolytics.

## Respiratory Diseases

### Chronic Conditions
- **Asthma**: Inflamed, narrowed airways. Symptoms: wheezing, coughing, shortness of breath. Diagnosed by spirometry. Treated with inhalers (bronchodilators, corticosteroids).
- **Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)**: Emphysema and chronic bronchitis. Symptoms: shortness of breath, chronic cough. Diagnosed by spirometry. Treated with bronchodilators, oxygen therapy.
- **Pulmonary Fibrosis**: Lung tissue scarring. Symptoms: progressive shortness of breath. Diagnosed by CT scan, lung biopsy. Treated with antifibrotics, oxygen.

### Acute Conditions
- **Acute Bronchitis**: Inflammation of bronchi. Symptoms: cough, mucus, fatigue. Usually viral. Treated with rest, fluids, cough suppressants.
- **Pneumothorax**: Collapsed lung. Symptoms: sudden chest pain, shortness of breath. Diagnosed by chest X-ray. Treated with needle aspiration or chest tube.

## Gastrointestinal Diseases

### Inflammatory Conditions
- **Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)**: Stomach acid reflux. Symptoms: heartburn, regurgitation. Diagnosed by endoscopy. Treated with PPIs, lifestyle changes.
- **Peptic Ulcer Disease**: Stomach or duodenal ulcers. Symptoms: abdominal pain, bleeding. Diagnosed by endoscopy. Treated with PPIs, antibiotics (if H. pylori).
- **Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)**: Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis. Symptoms: abdominal pain, diarrhea, weight loss. Diagnosed by colonoscopy. Treated with anti-inflammatories, immunosuppressants.
- **Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)**: Functional GI disorder. Symptoms: abdominal pain, bloating, irregular bowel habits. Diagnosed by Rome criteria. Treated with fiber, antispasmodics.

### Liver Diseases
- **Hepatitis C**: HCV infection. Symptoms: fatigue, jaundice. Diagnosed by blood tests. Treated with direct-acting antivirals.
- **Cirrhosis**: Liver scarring from various causes. Symptoms: jaundice, ascites, confusion. Diagnosed by imaging, biopsy. Treated with lifestyle changes, transplant.
- **Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease (NAFLD)**: Fat accumulation in liver. Often asymptomatic. Diagnosed by ultrasound. Treated with weight loss, exercise.

## Endocrine and Metabolic Diseases

### Diabetes
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: Autoimmune destruction of beta cells. Symptoms: frequent urination, thirst, weight loss. Diagnosed by blood glucose. Treated with insulin injections.
- **Type 2 Diabetes**: Insulin resistance. Symptoms: gradual onset, fatigue, blurred vision. Diagnosed by HbA1c. Treated with oral medications, insulin, lifestyle changes.
- **Gestational Diabetes**: Pregnancy-related insulin resistance. Symptoms: often asymptomatic. Diagnosed by glucose tolerance test. Treated with diet, insulin if needed.

### Thyroid Disorders
- **Hypothyroidism**: Underactive thyroid. Symptoms: fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance. Diagnosed by TSH levels. Treated with levothyroxine.
- **Hyperthyroidism**: Overactive thyroid. Symptoms: weight loss, heat intolerance, rapid heartbeat. Diagnosed by TSH, T3/T4. Treated with antithyroid drugs, radioactive iodine.
- **Thyroid Cancer**: Malignant thyroid tumors. Symptoms: neck lump, hoarseness. Diagnosed by biopsy. Treated with surgery, radioactive iodine.

### Other Endocrine
- **Cushing's Syndrome**: Excess cortisol. Symptoms: weight gain, moon face, hypertension. Diagnosed by cortisol levels. Treated with surgery, medications.
- **Addison's Disease**: Adrenal insufficiency. Symptoms: fatigue, weight loss, hypotension. Diagnosed by ACTH stimulation. Treated with corticosteroids.

## Neurological Diseases

### Degenerative Diseases
- **Alzheimer's Disease**: Progressive dementia. Symptoms: memory loss, confusion, behavioral changes. Diagnosed by cognitive tests, imaging. Treated with cholinesterase inhibitors.
- **Parkinson's Disease**: Dopamine neuron loss. Symptoms: tremor, rigidity, bradykinesia. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with levodopa, dopamine agonists.
- **Multiple Sclerosis (MS)**: Autoimmune demyelination. Symptoms: weakness, numbness, vision problems. Diagnosed by MRI, lumbar puncture. Treated with disease-modifying therapies.
- **Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)**: Motor neuron degeneration. Symptoms: muscle weakness, difficulty speaking. Diagnosed by EMG, clinical exam. Treated with riluzole, supportive care.

### Cerebrovascular Diseases
- **Migraine**: Recurrent headaches. Symptoms: throbbing pain, nausea, sensitivity to light. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with triptans, preventive medications.
- **Epilepsy**: Recurrent seizures. Symptoms: convulsions, loss of consciousness. Diagnosed by EEG. Treated with anticonvulsants.
- **Bell's Palsy**: Facial nerve paralysis. Symptoms: sudden facial weakness. Often idiopathic. Treated with corticosteroids, physical therapy.

## Mental Health Conditions

### Mood Disorders
- **Major Depressive Disorder**: Persistent sadness. Symptoms: depressed mood, anhedonia, sleep/appetite changes. Diagnosed by DSM-5 criteria. Treated with antidepressants, therapy.
- **Bipolar Disorder**: Mood swings between mania and depression. Symptoms: manic episodes, depressive episodes. Diagnosed by clinical history. Treated with mood stabilizers.
- **Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)**: Winter depression. Symptoms: seasonal mood changes. Diagnosed by pattern. Treated with light therapy, antidepressants.

### Anxiety Disorders
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)**: Excessive worry. Symptoms: restlessness, fatigue, concentration problems. Diagnosed by clinical criteria. Treated with SSRIs, therapy.
- **Panic Disorder**: Sudden panic attacks. Symptoms: chest pain, shortness of breath, fear. Diagnosed by clinical history. Treated with SSRIs, benzodiazepines.
- **Social Anxiety**: Fear of social situations. Symptoms: avoidance, sweating, trembling. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with SSRIs, CBT.
- **Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)**: Trauma response. Symptoms: flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance. Diagnosed by clinical criteria. Treated with trauma-focused therapy.

### Other Mental Health
- **Schizophrenia**: Psychotic disorder. Symptoms: hallucinations, delusions, disorganized thinking. Diagnosed by DSM-5. Treated with antipsychotics, therapy.
- **Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)**: Inattention and hyperactivity. Symptoms: poor focus, impulsivity. Diagnosed by clinical criteria. Treated with stimulants, behavioral therapy.
- **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)**: Obsessions and compulsions. Symptoms: repetitive thoughts/behaviors. Diagnosed clinically. Treated with SSRIs, CBT.

## Musculoskeletal Diseases

### Arthritis
- **Osteoarthritis**: Joint cartilage degeneration. Symptoms: joint pain, stiffness. Diagnosed by X-rays. Treated with NSAIDs, physical therapy.
- **Rheumatoid Arthritis**: Autoimmune joint inflammation. Symptoms: swollen, painful joints. Diagnosed by blood tests, imaging. Treated with DMARDs.
- **Gout**: Uric acid crystal deposition. Symptoms: severe joint pain, swelling. Diagnosed by joint fluid analysis. Treated with NSAIDs, colchicine.

### Bone Diseases
- **Osteoporosis**: Low bone density. Symptoms: often asymptomatic until fracture. Diagnosed by DEXA scan. Treated with bisphosphonates, calcium, vitamin D.
- **Paget's Disease**: Abnormal bone remodeling. Symptoms: bone pain, deformities. Diagnosed by X-rays, alkaline phosphatase. Treated with bisphosphonates.

## Cancer

### Major Types
- **Breast Cancer**: Malignant breast tumors. Symptoms: lump, nipple changes, pain. Diagnosed by mammogram, biopsy. Treated with surgery, chemotherapy, radiation.
- **Lung Cancer**: Malignant lung tumors. Symptoms: cough, weight loss, chest pain. Diagnosed by CT, biopsy. Treated with surgery, chemotherapy, targeted therapy.
- **Colorectal Cancer**: Colon or rectal tumors. Symptoms: blood in stool, abdominal pain. Diagnosed by colonoscopy. Treated with surgery, chemotherapy.
- **Prostate Cancer**: Prostate gland tumors. Symptoms: urinary problems, bone pain. Diagnosed by PSA test, biopsy. Treated with surgery, radiation, hormone therapy.
- **Skin Cancer (Melanoma)**: Malignant skin tumors. Symptoms: changing mole, irregular borders. Diagnosed by biopsy. Treated with surgery, immunotherapy.

### Blood Cancers
- **Leukemia**: Blood cell malignancies. Symptoms: fatigue, infections, bleeding. Diagnosed by blood counts, bone marrow biopsy. Treated with chemotherapy, stem cell transplant.
- **Lymphoma**: Lymphatic system cancers. Symptoms: swollen lymph nodes, fever, weight loss. Diagnosed by biopsy. Treated with chemotherapy, radiation.
- **Multiple Myeloma**: Plasma cell cancer. Symptoms: bone pain, fatigue, infections. Diagnosed by blood tests, imaging. Treated with chemotherapy, stem cell transplant.

## Autoimmune Diseases

### Systemic
- **Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)**: Autoimmune inflammation. Symptoms: joint pain, rashes, fatigue. Diagnosed by ANA test. Treated with immunosuppressants.
- **Rheumatoid Arthritis**: Joint inflammation. Symptoms: swollen joints, morning stiffness. Diagnosed by RF, anti-CCP. Treated with DMARDs.
- **Scleroderma**: Skin and organ fibrosis. Symptoms: skin thickening, Raynaud's. Diagnosed by clinical exam, autoantibodies. Treated with immunosuppressants.

### Organ-Specific
- **Type 1 Diabetes**: Pancreatic beta cell destruction. Symptoms: thirst, frequent urination. Diagnosed by autoantibodies. Treated with insulin.
- **Graves' Disease**: Thyroid autoimmunity. Symptoms: weight loss, rapid heartbeat. Diagnosed by TSH receptor antibodies. Treated with antithyroid drugs.
- **Myasthenia Gravis**: Neuromuscular junction antibodies. Symptoms: muscle weakness. Diagnosed by acetylcholine receptor antibodies. Treated with immunosuppressants.

## Genetic and Congenital Conditions

### Chromosomal Disorders
- **Down Syndrome**: Trisomy 21. Symptoms: intellectual disability, characteristic features. Diagnosed by karyotype. Managed with supportive care.
- **Turner Syndrome**: Monosomy X. Symptoms: short stature, infertility. Diagnosed by karyotype. Treated with growth hormone.
- **Klinefelter Syndrome**: XXY karyotype. Symptoms: tall stature, infertility. Diagnosed by karyotype. Treated with testosterone.

### Single Gene Disorders
- **Cystic Fibrosis**: CFTR gene mutation. Symptoms: lung infections, digestive problems. Diagnosed by sweat test. Treated with enzyme replacement, antibiotics.
- **Sickle Cell Disease**: Hemoglobin S mutation. Symptoms: pain crises, anemia. Diagnosed by hemoglobin electrophoresis. Treated with hydroxyurea, transfusions.
- **Huntington's Disease**: CAG repeat expansion. Symptoms: movement disorders, dementia. Diagnosed by genetic testing. Managed symptomatically.

### Congenital Anomalies
- **Congenital Heart Disease**: Heart defects present at birth. Symptoms vary by type. Diagnosed by echocardiogram. Treated with surgery, medications.
- **Spina Bifida**: Neural tube defect. Symptoms: paralysis, hydrocephalus. Diagnosed prenatally. Treated with surgery, physical therapy.
- **Cleft Lip/Palate**: Facial malformation. Symptoms: feeding/speech difficulties. Diagnosed at birth. Treated with surgery.

Understanding these conditions helps in early recognition, proper diagnosis, and effective treatment strategies for better health outcomes.

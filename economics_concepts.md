# Economics Concepts

Economics studies how societies allocate scarce resources to satisfy unlimited wants and needs.

## Basic Economic Principles

### Scarcity and Choice
- **Scarcity**: Limited resources, unlimited wants
- **Opportunity Cost**: Value of next best alternative
- **Trade-offs**: Choosing between alternatives
- **Production Possibilities**: Maximum output combinations
- **Economic Efficiency**: Optimal resource allocation

### Supply and Demand
- **Demand**: Quantity wanted at different prices
- **Supply**: Quantity offered at different prices
- **Equilibrium**: Market clearing price
- **Elasticity**: Responsiveness to price changes
- **Market Forces**: Price determination mechanism

### Marginal Analysis
- **Marginal Cost**: Additional cost of one more unit
- **Marginal Benefit**: Additional benefit of one more unit
- **Marginal Revenue**: Additional revenue from one more unit
- **Optimization**: Where marginal cost equals marginal benefit
- **Decision Making**: Incremental analysis approach

## Microeconomics

### Market Structures
- **Perfect Competition**: Many buyers/sellers, identical products
- **Monopolistic Competition**: Many sellers, differentiated products
- **Oligopoly**: Few large sellers
- **Monopoly**: Single seller, no close substitutes
- **Natural Monopoly**: High fixed costs, economies of scale

### Consumer Theory
- **Utility**: Satisfaction from consumption
- **Budget Constraint**: Income limitation
- **Indifference Curves**: Preference combinations
- **Consumer Surplus**: Benefit above price paid
- **Substitution Effect**: Relative price changes

### Producer Theory
- **Production Function**: Input-output relationship
- **Cost Curves**: Short-run and long-run costs
- **Profit Maximization**: Marginal revenue = marginal cost
- **Economies of Scale**: Cost advantages of size
- **Economies of Scope**: Cost advantages of variety

### Market Failures
- **Externalities**: Unintended third-party effects
- **Public Goods**: Non-excludable, non-rivalrous
- **Information Asymmetry**: Unequal information access
- **Market Power**: Ability to influence prices
- **Incomplete Markets**: Missing risk-sharing mechanisms

## Macroeconomics

### National Income Accounting
- **GDP**: Gross Domestic Product (output measure)
- **GNP**: Gross National Product (citizen income)
- **National Income**: Factor payments to residents
- **Personal Income**: Disposable income available
- **Net National Product**: Depreciation adjustment

### Economic Growth
- **Real GDP Growth**: Inflation-adjusted expansion
- **Productivity**: Output per worker hour
- **Capital Accumulation**: Investment in physical capital
- **Technological Progress**: Innovation and efficiency
- **Human Capital**: Education and skills investment

### Business Cycles
- **Expansion**: Economic growth period
- **Peak**: Cycle high point
- **Contraction**: Economic decline
- **Trough**: Cycle low point
- **Recovery**: Return to expansion

### Inflation and Unemployment
- **Inflation**: General price level increase
- **Deflation**: General price level decrease
- **Unemployment Types**: Frictional, structural, cyclical
- **Phillips Curve**: Inflation-unemployment trade-off
- **Natural Rate**: Long-run unemployment equilibrium

### Monetary Policy
- **Central Banks**: Federal Reserve, ECB, Bank of Japan
- **Money Supply**: M1, M2, M3 measures
- **Interest Rates**: Federal funds rate, discount rate
- **Open Market Operations**: Buying/selling securities
- **Quantitative Easing**: Large-scale asset purchases

### Fiscal Policy
- **Government Spending**: Public expenditure programs
- **Taxation**: Revenue collection methods
- **Budget Deficit/Surplus**: Government borrowing/lending
- **Automatic Stabilizers**: Built-in economic responses
- **Discretionary Policy**: Active government intervention

## International Economics

### Trade Theory
- **Comparative Advantage**: Specialization benefits
- **Absolute Advantage**: Production superiority
- **Terms of Trade**: Export-import price ratios
- **Balance of Trade**: Exports minus imports
- **Balance of Payments**: All international transactions

### Exchange Rates
- **Fixed Exchange Rates**: Government-set currency values
- **Floating Exchange Rates**: Market-determined values
- **Purchasing Power Parity**: Currency value equalization
- **Interest Rate Parity**: Investment return equalization
- **Currency Crises**: Sudden exchange rate changes

### International Institutions
- **IMF**: International Monetary Fund (lending, stability)
- **World Bank**: Development financing
- **WTO**: World Trade Organization (trade rules)
- **BIS**: Bank for International Settlements
- **Regional Blocs**: EU, NAFTA, ASEAN

## Development Economics

### Economic Development
- **Human Development Index**: Health, education, income
- **Poverty Measurement**: Absolute vs relative poverty
- **Inequality Metrics**: Gini coefficient, Lorenz curve
- **Sustainable Development**: Economic, social, environmental
- **Inclusive Growth**: Broad-based prosperity

### Growth Theories
- **Harrod-Domar**: Savings-investment relationship
- **Solow Model**: Capital accumulation and technology
- **Endogenous Growth**: Innovation-driven expansion
- **Dependency Theory**: Rich-poor nation relationships
- **Stages of Growth**: Rostow's development phases

### Development Strategies
- **Import Substitution**: Domestic industry protection
- **Export Promotion**: International market focus
- **Structural Adjustment**: IMF/World Bank programs
- **Foreign Aid**: Development assistance
- **Microfinance**: Small-scale lending

## Behavioral Economics

### Decision Making
- **Bounded Rationality**: Limited information processing
- **Prospect Theory**: Loss aversion, reference points
- **Mental Accounting**: Categorization of expenditures
- **Anchoring**: Initial information influence
- **Framing Effects**: Presentation impact on choices

### Market Psychology
- **Herd Behavior**: Following crowd decisions
- **Overconfidence**: Excessive self-assurance
- **Confirmation Bias**: Seeking supporting information
- **Availability Heuristic**: Recent event overemphasis
- **Status Quo Bias**: Preference for current state

## Environmental Economics

### Externalities and Valuation
- **Negative Externalities**: Pollution, congestion
- **Positive Externalities**: Education, vaccination
- **Cost-Benefit Analysis**: Project evaluation
- **Contingent Valuation**: Willingness-to-pay surveys
- **Hedonic Pricing**: Market value of environmental features

### Resource Economics
- **Renewable Resources**: Fisheries, forests, groundwater
- **Non-renewable Resources**: Oil, minerals, fossil fuels
- **Common Pool Resources**: Shared access problems
- **Tragedy of the Commons**: Overuse incentives
- **Sustainable Yield**: Maximum sustainable harvest

### Climate Economics
- **Carbon Pricing**: Tax or cap-and-trade systems
- **Discount Rates**: Future value calculations
- **Green GDP**: Environmental cost adjustments
- **Adaptation Costs**: Climate change response
- **Transition Economics**: Low-carbon economy shift

## Labor Economics

### Labor Markets
- **Labor Supply**: Hours worked decisions
- **Labor Demand**: Employment level determination
- **Wage Determination**: Market clearing mechanisms
- **Human Capital**: Education and training investment
- **Labor Mobility**: Geographic and occupational movement

### Wage Theories
- **Marginal Productivity**: Value of worker contribution
- **Efficiency Wages**: Above-market compensation
- **Minimum Wage**: Government wage floors
- **Unions**: Collective bargaining power
- **Discrimination**: Unequal treatment effects

## Public Economics

### Public Goods
- **Non-excludability**: Consumption prevention impossible
- **Non-rivalry**: One person's use doesn't reduce others'
- **Free Rider Problem**: Benefit without contribution
- **Government Provision**: Public sector role
- **Club Goods**: Limited access public goods

### Taxation
- **Progressive Taxation**: Higher rates for higher income
- **Regressive Taxation**: Lower rates for higher income
- **Value-Added Tax**: Consumption taxation
- **Corporate Taxation**: Business profit taxation
- **Tax Incidence**: Who ultimately bears tax burden

### Government Spending
- **Transfer Payments**: Welfare, unemployment benefits
- **Public Investment**: Infrastructure, education
- **Defense Spending**: National security expenditures
- **Social Insurance**: Retirement, health programs
- **Subsidies**: Industry support payments

Economics provides frameworks for understanding resource allocation, market behavior, and policy impacts on society.

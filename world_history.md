# World History Overview

World history spans from ancient civilizations to modern times, encompassing the development of human societies, cultures, and civilizations.

## Ancient Civilizations

### Mesopotamia (c. 3500-500 BCE)
- Located in the Fertile Crescent (modern Iraq)
- Sumerians invented cuneiform writing (c. 3200 BCE)
- Code of Hammurabi (c. 1754 BCE) - earliest known legal code
- Contributions: wheel, plow, sailboat, mathematics, astronomy

### Ancient Egypt (c. 3100-30 BCE)
- Nile River civilization
- Pharaohs and pyramids
- Hieroglyphic writing
- Achievements: calendar, medicine, architecture
- Key figures: Cleopatra, Tutankhamun, Ramses II

### Indus Valley Civilization (c. 3300-1300 BCE)
- Located in modern Pakistan and northwest India
- Highly advanced urban planning
- Sophisticated drainage systems
- Undeciphered script (Harappan)

### Ancient China (c. 2070 BCE-1912 CE)
- Dynasties: Shang, Zhou, Qin, Han, Tang, Ming, Qing
- Inventions: paper, gunpowder, compass, printing
- Great Wall construction began
- Confucian philosophy

### Ancient Greece (c. 800-146 BCE)
- Birthplace of democracy (Athens)
- Philosophy: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle
- Mathematics: Pythagoras, Euclid
- Olympic Games originated
- Key figures: Alexander the Great, Pericles

### Roman Empire (c. 27 BCE-476 CE)
- Republic to Empire
- Engineering: roads, aqueducts, concrete
- Law and governance systems
- Spread of Christianity
- Fall led to Middle Ages

## Middle Ages (c. 500-1500 CE)

### Byzantine Empire
- Continuation of Eastern Roman Empire
- Preserved Greek and Roman knowledge
- Orthodox Christianity
- Constantinople as center of trade

### Islamic Golden Age (c. 750-1258 CE)
- House of Wisdom in Baghdad
- Advancements in mathematics, astronomy, medicine
- Scholars: Al-Khwarizmi, Ibn Sina (Avicenna), Al-Razi
- Preservation of ancient Greek texts

### Medieval Europe
- Feudal system
- Crusades (1096-1291)
- Black Death (1347-1351) - killed 30-60% of population
- Renaissance begins in Italy (14th century)

## Age of Exploration (15th-18th centuries)

### European Expansion
- Portuguese and Spanish exploration
- Christopher Columbus reaches Americas (1492)
- Vasco da Gama reaches India (1498)
- Magellan circumnavigates globe (1519-1522)

### Colonial Empires
- British, French, Spanish, Dutch colonies
- Transatlantic slave trade
- Columbian Exchange: goods, diseases, cultures
- Impact on indigenous populations

## Industrial Revolution (c. 1760-1840)

### Technological Innovations
- Steam engine (James Watt)
- Factory system
- Textile machinery
- Iron and steel production
- Railroads and canals

### Social Changes
- Urbanization
- Rise of working class
- Child labor issues
- Labor unions emerge
- Capitalism vs socialism debates

## 19th Century Developments

### Unifications and Revolutions
- Italian unification (1861)
- German unification (1871)
- American Civil War (1861-1865)
- Abolition of slavery
- Women's suffrage movements begin

### Imperialism
- Scramble for Africa
- British Raj in India
- Opium Wars in China
- Boxer Rebellion
- Height of European colonialism

## 20th Century

### World Wars
- World War I (1914-1918): trench warfare, new weapons
- Interwar period: Great Depression, rise of fascism
- World War II (1939-1945): Holocaust, atomic bombs
- Cold War (1947-1991): US vs USSR

### Decolonization
- Independence movements in Asia and Africa
- Partition of India (1947)
- End of European empires
- Formation of United Nations (1945)

### Technological Revolution
- Nuclear energy
- Space race
- Computer revolution
- Internet development
- Medical breakthroughs

## 21st Century

### Globalization
- Economic interdependence
- International organizations: WTO, IMF
- Climate change awareness
- Digital revolution
- Social media impact

### Major Events
- 9/11 attacks (2001)
- Financial crisis (2008)
- Arab Spring (2010-2012)
- COVID-19 pandemic (2019-2021)
- Russia-Ukraine conflict (2022-present)

### Current Trends
- Artificial intelligence development
- Renewable energy transition
- Demographic shifts
- Geopolitical realignments
- Space exploration resurgence

# Physics Fundamentals

Physics is the fundamental science that studies matter, energy, motion, and the fundamental forces of nature.

## Classical Mechanics

### Newton's Laws of Motion
1. **First Law (Inertia)**: An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an unbalanced force
2. **Second Law (F = ma)**: Force equals mass times acceleration
3. **Third Law**: For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction

### Energy and Work
- **Kinetic Energy**: Energy of motion (1/2 mv²)
- **Potential Energy**: Stored energy (mgh for gravitational)
- **Work**: Force times distance (W = Fd)
- **Power**: Work per unit time (P = W/t)
- Conservation of energy

### Momentum
- Linear momentum: p = mv
- Conservation of momentum
- Impulse: change in momentum (J = Δp)

## Thermodynamics

### Laws of Thermodynamics
1. **First Law**: Energy conservation (ΔU = Q - W)
2. **Second Law**: Entropy increases in isolated systems
3. **Third Law**: Absolute zero is unattainable
4. **Zeroth Law**: Thermal equilibrium

### Heat Transfer
- **Conduction**: Direct molecular interaction
- **Convection**: Fluid motion carries heat
- **Radiation**: Electromagnetic waves

### Phase Changes
- Melting, freezing, vaporization, condensation
- Specific heat capacity
- Latent heat

## Electricity and Magnetism

### Electric Charge and Fields
- Coulomb's law: F = kq₁q₂/r²
- Electric field: E = F/q
- Electric potential: V = kq/r
- Capacitance: C = Q/V

### Electric Circuits
- Ohm's law: V = IR
- Series and parallel circuits
- Power: P = VI = I²R = V²/R
- AC vs DC current

### Magnetism
- Magnetic fields and forces
- Right-hand rule
- Electromagnetic induction (Faraday's law)
- Lenz's law

## Waves and Optics

### Wave Properties
- Wavelength, frequency, amplitude
- Speed = frequency × wavelength
- Interference and diffraction
- Standing waves

### Light and Optics
- Reflection and refraction
- Snell's law: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂
- Mirrors: concave, convex
- Lenses: converging, diverging
- Wave-particle duality

## Modern Physics

### Special Relativity (Einstein)
- Speed of light is constant
- Time dilation: t = t₀/√(1 - v²/c²)
- Length contraction: L = L₀√(1 - v²/c²)
- E = mc² (mass-energy equivalence)

### Quantum Mechanics
- Wave-particle duality
- Uncertainty principle: ΔxΔp ≥ ħ/2
- Quantum numbers
- Schrödinger equation
- Quantum tunneling

### Atomic Physics
- Bohr model
- Quantum mechanical model
- Electron configurations
- Spectroscopy

### Nuclear Physics
- Nuclear structure: protons, neutrons
- Radioactivity: alpha, beta, gamma decay
- Nuclear reactions: fission, fusion
- Half-life and decay constants

## Particle Physics

### Standard Model
- Fundamental particles: quarks, leptons, bosons
- Forces: strong, weak, electromagnetic, gravity
- Higgs boson and mass
- Particle accelerators

### Cosmology
- Big Bang theory
- Expansion of universe
- Cosmic microwave background
- Dark matter and dark energy

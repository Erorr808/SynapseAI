## Inheritance Patterns

### Autosomal Dominant
- Single copy expression
- Vertical inheritance pattern
- Carriers absent generally
- Examples: Huntington's disease
- Penetrance considerations

### Autosomal Recessive
- Two copies required
- Carrier state possible
- Horizontal inheritance pattern
- Examples: Cystic fibrosis
- Population carrier frequency

### Sex-Linked Inheritance
- X-chromosome located genes
- Y-chromosome linked traits
- Sex-specific inheritance patterns
- Carrier females
- Affected males disproportionately

### Complex Inheritance
- Multiple genes involved
- Environmental factors
- Polygenic traits
- Quantitative variation
- Threshold models

## Mutations

### Types of Mutations
- Point mutations: Single base changes
- Insertions: DNA sequence additions
- Deletions: DNA sequence removals
- Duplications: Repeated sequences
- Inversions: Reversed sequences
- Translocations: Moved segments

### Mutation Causes
- Spontaneous errors
- Environmental mutagens
- Radiation exposure
- Chemical carcinogens
- Viral insertion

### Mutation Effects
- Beneficial: Adaptive advantage
- Neutral: No fitness change
- Deleterious: Fitness reduction
- Silent: No phenotype change
- Expression altering

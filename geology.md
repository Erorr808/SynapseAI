# Geology Fundamentals

Geology is the study of Earth's physical structure, substances, processes, and history.

## Earth's Structure

### Layers of Earth
- **Crust**: Outermost layer, 5-70 km thick
  - Oceanic crust: Dense, thin, basalt composition
  - Continental crust: Less dense, thick, granite composition
- **Mantle**: 2,900 km thick, mostly solid rock
  - Upper mantle: Rigid, part of tectonic plates
  - Lower mantle: More plastic, convecting
- **Outer Core**: 2,200 km thick, liquid iron-nickel
- **Inner Core**: 1,200 km radius, solid iron-nickel

### Plate Tectonics
- **Theory**: Earth's lithosphere divided into plates
- **Types of Boundaries**:
  - Divergent: Plates move apart (mid-ocean ridges)
  - Convergent: Plates collide (mountains, trenches)
  - Transform: Plates slide past each other (faults)
- **Driving Forces**: Mantle convection, slab pull, ridge push

## Rocks and Minerals

### Rock Cycle
- **Igneous**: Formed from cooled magma/lava
  - Intrusive (plutonic): Slow cooling underground
  - Extrusive (volcanic): Fast cooling at surface
- **Sedimentary**: Formed from sediment compaction
  - Clastic: Fragments cemented together
  - Chemical: Minerals precipitate from solution
  - Organic: Remains of organisms
- **Metamorphic**: Changed by heat/pressure
  - Foliated: Minerals aligned in layers
  - Non-foliated: Minerals recrystallized

### Common Minerals
- **Quartz**: SiO₂, hardest common mineral
- **Feldspar**: Most abundant mineral group
- **Mica**: Sheet-like cleavage
- **Calcite**: Reacts with acid, forms limestone
- **Pyrite**: "Fool's gold," FeS₂
- **Galena**: Lead ore, PbS

### Mineral Properties
- **Hardness**: Mohs scale (1-10)
- **Luster**: Metallic vs non-metallic
- **Cleavage**: How minerals break
- **Specific Gravity**: Density relative to water
- **Color/Streak**: Appearance and powder color

## Geological Time

### Geological Time Scale
- **Eons**: Hadean, Archean, Proterozoic, Phanerozoic
- **Eras**: Paleozoic, Mesozoic, Cenozoic
- **Periods**: Cambrian, Ordovician, Silurian, Devonian, Carboniferous, Permian, Triassic, Jurassic, Cretaceous, Paleogene, Neogene, Quaternary
- **Epochs**: Pleistocene, Holocene (current)

### Major Geological Events
- **Precambrian**: Formation of continents, early life
- **Paleozoic**: Ancient life explosion, Pangaea formation
- **Mesozoic**: Age of dinosaurs, Pangaea breakup
- **Cenozoic**: Rise of mammals, human evolution

### Dating Methods
- **Relative Dating**: Law of superposition, cross-cutting relationships
- **Absolute Dating**: Radiometric dating
  - Carbon-14: Up to 50,000 years
  - Uranium-lead: Billions of years
  - Potassium-argon: Millions of years

## Earth Processes

### Weathering and Erosion
- **Mechanical Weathering**: Physical breakdown
  - Freeze-thaw, root wedging, exfoliation
- **Chemical Weathering**: Mineral alteration
  - Dissolution, oxidation, hydrolysis
- **Erosion**: Transport of weathered material
  - Water, wind, ice, gravity

### Deposition
- **Sedimentary Environments**:
  - Rivers: Point bars, floodplains
  - Lakes: Varved sediments
  - Oceans: Continental shelf, deep sea
  - Deserts: Sand dunes, alluvial fans
  - Glaciers: Moraines, drumlins

### Mass Wasting
- **Types**: Falls, slides, flows, creep
- **Triggers**: Heavy rain, earthquakes, human activity
- **Prevention**: Retaining walls, drainage, vegetation

## Natural Resources

### Energy Resources
- **Fossil Fuels**: Coal, oil, natural gas
  - Formation: Organic matter burial and transformation
  - Reserves: Finite, non-renewable
- **Nuclear**: Uranium deposits
- **Renewable**: Geothermal, hydroelectric potential

### Mineral Resources
- **Metallic Ores**: Iron, copper, gold, silver
- **Industrial Minerals**: Limestone, gypsum, salt
- **Gemstones**: Diamonds, rubies, emeralds
- **Rare Earth Elements**: Used in electronics

### Water Resources
- **Groundwater**: Aquifers, wells, springs
- **Surface Water**: Rivers, lakes, reservoirs
- **Water Quality**: Contamination sources and treatment

## Geological Hazards

### Earthquakes
- **Causes**: Tectonic plate movement, fault rupture
- **Measurement**: Richter scale (magnitude), Mercalli scale (intensity)
- **Effects**: Ground shaking, liquefaction, tsunamis
- **Prediction**: Seismic gaps, animal behavior (unreliable)

### Volcanoes
- **Types**: Shield, cinder cone, composite (stratovolcano)
- **Eruptions**: Effusive (lava flows), explosive (ash clouds)
- **Hazards**: Lava flows, pyroclastic flows, lahars
- **Benefits**: Fertile soil, geothermal energy, tourism

### Landslides
- **Types**: Rock falls, debris flows, earth flows
- **Causes**: Steep slopes, heavy rain, earthquakes
- **Prevention**: Slope stabilization, drainage systems
- **Monitoring**: Tiltmeters, extensometers

## Environmental Geology

### Soil Formation
- **Factors**: Parent material, climate, organisms, topography, time
- **Horizons**: O (organic), A (topsoil), B (subsoil), C (parent), R (bedrock)
- **Soil Types**: Entisols, Inceptisols, Alfisols, Mollisols, etc.

### Coastal Geology
- **Processes**: Wave erosion, longshore drift, deposition
- **Features**: Beaches, dunes, barrier islands, estuaries
- **Sea Level Change**: Eustatic (global), isostatic (local)
- **Coastal Management**: Hard engineering, soft engineering

### Climate Change Geology
- **Paleoclimate**: Ice cores, sediment records
- **Carbon Cycle**: Geological carbon sequestration
- **Ocean Acidification**: Coral dissolution, shellfish impacts
- **Permafrost Thawing**: Methane release, coastal erosion

Geology provides essential knowledge for resource management, hazard mitigation, and environmental protection.
